# Kanto Region — Pokemon Crystal Location Reference

> Source: pokecrystal disassembly (kanto_grass.asm, landmarks.asm) + Bulbapedia
> Game: Pokemon Crystal (Gen II)
> Purpose: Comprehensive Kanto location data for vector database embedding
> Note: Kanto is the post-game region, accessible after defeating the Elite Four

---

## Overview

After becoming Champion in Johto, Professor Oak grants access to Kanto via the S.S. Aqua from Olivine City to Vermilion City (or Magnet Train from Goldenrod to Saffron after restoring power). Three years have passed since the events of Red/Blue/Yellow, and Kanto has changed significantly.

### Key Differences from Gen 1
- Viridian Forest has been cut down (no longer explorable)
- Cinnabar Island was destroyed by a volcanic eruption; Blaine relocated to Seafoam Islands
- Safari Zone in Fuchsia City is closed
- Silph Co. is no longer explorable (just the lobby)
- Pokemon Mansion on Cinnabar is destroyed
- Cerulean Cave is accessible (Mewtwo is NOT present; just wild Pokemon)
- Power Plant was shut down; must restore it (Kanto sidequest)
- S.S. Anne is no longer in Vermilion Harbor
- Lavender Town's Pokemon Tower has been converted to a Radio Tower
- Many new trainers have replaced the originals
- Gym Leaders have stronger teams (levels 42-56)
- Time-of-day wild encounters added

## Kanto Cities and Towns

### Pallet Town
- **Connections**: North to Route 1; South to Route 21 (Surf)
- **Buildings**: Player Red's House, Professor Oak's Lab, Rival Blue's House
- **Key NPCs**: Professor Oak (evaluates Pokedex), Daisy Oak (grooms Pokemon for happiness; daily)
- **Notes**: Smaller than Johto's starting town; Red's mother is still home

### Viridian City
- **Connections**: South to Route 1; North to Route 2; West to Route 22
- **Buildings**: Pokemon Center, Poke Mart, Viridian Gym, Trainer House
- **Gym**: Blue (rival from Gen 1) — Pidgeot Lv56, Alakazam Lv54, Rhydon Lv56, Gyarados Lv58, Exeggutor Lv55, Arcanine Lv58 — Earth Badge
- **Trainer House**: Daily battle against a trainer team (default: Cal with Meganium/Typhlosion/Feraligatr Lv50)
- **Notes**: Blue is the Gym Leader (no type specialty). He was Champion in Gen 1.
- **Fly Point**: Yes

### Pewter City
- **Connections**: South to Route 2; East to Route 3
- **Buildings**: Pokemon Center, Poke Mart, Pewter Gym, Museum
- **Gym**: Brock — Graveler Lv41, Rhyhorn Lv41, Omastar Lv42, Kabutops Lv42, Onix Lv44 — Boulder Badge
- **Key NPCs**: Brock (Gym Leader), Museum Old Man (gives Silver Wing in Gold / Rainbow Wing in Silver after all 16 badges; but NOT in Crystal — different method)
- **Fly Point**: Yes

### Cerulean City
- **Connections**: South to Route 5; East to Route 9; North to Route 24/25; West to Route 4
- **Buildings**: Pokemon Center, Poke Mart, Cerulean Gym, Bike Shop (closed)
- **Gym**: Misty — Golduck Lv42, Quagsire Lv42, Lapras Lv44, Starmie Lv47 — Cascade Badge. Misty is initially absent (found on a date at Route 25/Cape; must scare away her date to make her return).
- **Key Location**: Cerulean Cave (northwest, Surf required)
- **Story**: Team Rocket Grunt steals Machine Part from Power Plant; must be chased and defeated
- **Fly Point**: Yes

### Vermilion City
- **Connections**: North to Route 6; East to Route 11; South via S.S. Aqua port
- **Buildings**: Pokemon Center, Poke Mart, Vermilion Gym, Pokemon Fan Club, Diglett's Cave entrance, S.S. Aqua port
- **Gym**: Lt. Surge — Voltorb Lv44, Electrode Lv40, Magneton Lv40, Electabuzz Lv46, Electrode Lv40 — Thunder Badge. Trash can switch puzzle.
- **Key NPCs**: Pokemon Fan Club Chairman (gives Lost Item → return to Copycat in Saffron)
- **S.S. Aqua**: Travels between Olivine City and Vermilion City (Wed + Sun departures)
- **Fly Point**: Yes

### Lavender Town
- **Connections**: West to Route 8; South to Route 12; North to Route 10
- **Buildings**: Pokemon Center, Poke Mart, Lavender Radio Tower (formerly Pokemon Tower), Name Rater, Mr. Fuji's House, Soul House
- **Radio Tower**: Replace the Radio Card with EXPN Card for Kanto radio stations (Poke Flute channel wakes Snorlax)
- **Notes**: Pokemon Tower no longer exists as a dungeon; it was converted to a Radio Tower
- **Fly Point**: Yes

### Celadon City
- **Connections**: East to Route 7; West to Route 16
- **Buildings**: Pokemon Center, Celadon Department Store (6F), Celadon Gym, Game Corner, Celadon Mansion, Restaurant
- **Gym**: Erika — Tangela Lv42, Jumpluff Lv41, Victreebel Lv46, Bellossom Lv46 — Rainbow Badge
- **Department Store**: Largest shop; includes TMs, evolution stones, stat boosters
- **Game Corner**: Still operational; prizes include Porygon, Larvitar (Crystal)
- **Fly Point**: Yes

### Saffron City
- **Connections**: North to Route 5; South to Route 6; East to Route 8; West to Route 7
- **Buildings**: Pokemon Center, Poke Mart, Saffron Gym, Silph Co. (lobby only), Fighting Dojo, Copycat's House, Magnet Train Station
- **Gym**: Sabrina — Espeon Lv46, Mr. Mime Lv46, Alakazam Lv48 — Marsh Badge. Teleporter warp puzzle.
- **Fighting Dojo**: Still present but no longer a pseudo-gym
- **Magnet Train**: High-speed rail to Goldenrod City (requires Pass from Copycat after returning her Lost Item/Poke Doll)
- **Copycat**: Lost her Poke Doll; give her one (from Fan Club in Vermilion) for the Pass
- **Fly Point**: Yes

### Fuchsia City
- **Connections**: East to Route 15; North to Route 18; South to Route 19; West to Route 14 (actually north)
- **Buildings**: Pokemon Center, Poke Mart, Fuchsia Gym, Safari Zone Gate (closed)
- **Gym**: Janine (Koga's daughter) — Crobat Lv36, Weezing Lv36, Ariados Lv33, Ariados Lv33, Venomoth Lv39 — Soul Badge
- **Safari Zone**: Closed. Warden is still around but the zone is inaccessible.
- **Notes**: Koga is now Elite Four member; Janine took over
- **Fly Point**: Yes

### Cinnabar Island
- **Connections**: North to Route 21 (Surf); East to Route 20 (Surf)
- **Buildings**: Pokemon Center only
- **Gym**: Blaine relocated to Seafoam Islands
- **Notes**: The island was devastated by a volcanic eruption. Only the Pokemon Center remains. Very small area.
- **Fly Point**: Yes

### Seafoam Islands
- **Connections**: Route 20
- **Gym**: Blaine — Magcargo Lv45, Magmar Lv45, Rapidash Lv50 — Volcano Badge. Located in a small cave area.
- **Wild Pokemon**: Zubat, Golbat, Seel, Dewgong, Psyduck, Golduck, Slowpoke, Slowbro, Jynx
- **Notes**: Much smaller dungeon than Gen 1; Blaine set up temporary gym here after eruption

### Indigo Plateau
- **Location**: End of Victory Road
- **Buildings**: Pokemon Center, Pokemon League HQ
- **Notes**: Same Elite Four as Johto storyline; rematches have higher levels
- **Fly Point**: Yes

## Kanto Dungeons

### Diglett's Cave
- **Location**: Between Route 2 and Route 11/Vermilion
- **Wild Pokemon**: Diglett Lv2-24 (morn/day: Lv2-24; night: Lv4-32), Dugtrio Lv16-32
- **Notes**: Unchanged from Gen 1; important shortcut between Vermilion and Pewter

### Mt. Moon
- **Location**: Between Route 3 and Route 4
- **Wild Pokemon**: Zubat Lv6, Geodude Lv8-10, Sandshrew Lv8, Paras Lv12, Clefairy Lv8-12
- **Special**: Monday night — Clefairy dance event (get Moon Stone)
- **Items**: Moon Stone (Monday event)

### Rock Tunnel
- **Location**: Route 9 to Route 10
- **Floors**: 2 (1F, B1F)
- **Wild Pokemon 1F**: Cubone Lv10, Geodude Lv11, Machop Lv12, Zubat Lv12, Machoke Lv15, Marowak Lv12; Night: Haunter Lv17
- **Wild Pokemon B1F**: Cubone Lv12, Geodude Lv14, Onix Lv16, Zubat Lv12, Marowak Lv15, Kangaskhan Lv15; Night: Haunter Lv15, Golbat Lv15
- **Notes**: Flash recommended; Kangaskhan is rare find here
- **Fly Point**: Yes (nearby)

### Victory Road (Kanto entrance)
- **Wild Pokemon**: Graveler Lv34, Rhyhorn Lv32, Onix Lv33, Golbat Lv34, Sandslash Lv35, Rhydon Lv35; Night: mostly Golbat/Graveler
- **Trainers**: Multiple Cooltrainers
- **Items**: TM26 Earthquake, Full Heal, HP Up, Max Revive

### Power Plant
- **Location**: Route 10 (Surf required)
- **Status**: Shut down; must find Machine Part (stolen by Rocket Grunt in Cerulean)
- **Sidequest**: Find Machine Part in Cerulean Gym (hidden in inner tubes), return to Power Plant Manager
- **Notes**: Zapdos does NOT appear here in Gen 2. No wild encounters inside.

### Cerulean Cave
- **Location**: Northwest of Cerulean City (Surf required)
- **Notes**: Mewtwo is NOT present in Gen 2. Cave has wild Pokemon only (Golduck, Parasect, Ditto).
- **Wild Pokemon**: Golbat, Parasect, Psyduck, Golduck, Ditto, Electrode, Misdreavus (night)

### Seafoam Islands
- **Location**: Route 20
- **Wild Pokemon**: Zubat, Golbat, Seel, Dewgong, Psyduck, Golduck, Slowpoke, Slowbro, Jynx
- **Gym**: Blaine's relocated gym is here (not the full dungeon from Gen 1)
- **Notes**: Articuno does NOT appear here in Gen 2

## Kanto Sidequests

### Power Plant Restoration
1. Visit Power Plant on Route 10 — Manager says Machine Part was stolen
2. Go to Cerulean City — find Rocket Grunt near gym
3. Defeat Grunt, he tells you where he hid it
4. Find Machine Part in Cerulean Gym (in the pool/inner tubes area)
5. Return to Power Plant Manager — power restored
6. Kanto Radio Tower in Lavender now operational
7. Get EXPN Card from Radio Tower — enables Poke Flute channel

### Snorlax Encounters
- Route 12 and Route 16 each have a Snorlax blocking the path
- Must use Poke Flute from radio (EXPN Card required)
- Snorlax is Lv50; only one of each

### Copycat's Poke Doll
1. Visit Copycat in Saffron — she lost her Poke Doll
2. Go to Pokemon Fan Club in Vermilion — Chairman has it (Lost Item)
3. Return Lost Item to Copycat — she gives Pass (Magnet Train access)

### Red Battle
- After obtaining all 16 badges, Mt. Silver entrance opens
- Climb to summit to battle Trainer Red
- Red's team: Pikachu Lv81, Espeon Lv73, Snorlax Lv75, Venusaur Lv77, Charizard Lv77, Blastoise Lv77
- The final challenge of the game; no dialogue, just battle
