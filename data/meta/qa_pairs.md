## Section 2: Mechanical Questions (Formulas, Interactions, Battle Flow)

### Q43
**Q:** How does the damage formula work in Gen 2?
**A:** The basic damage formula is: `Damage = ((2 * Level / 5 + 2) * Power * A/D) / 50 + 2) * Modifier`. Where A = Attack or Special Attack (depending on move type), D = Defense or Special Defense, Modifier = STAB (1.5x) * Type Effectiveness * Critical * Random(217-255)/255. Move type determines physical (Normal, Fighting, Poison, Ground, Flying, Bug, Rock, Ghost, Steel) or special (Fire, Water, Grass, Electric, Ice, Psychic, Dragon, Dark).
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q44
**Q:** How does the physical/special split work in Gen 2?
**A:** In Gen 2, whether a move is physical or special is determined by its TYPE, not the individual move. Physical types: Normal, Fighting, Poison, Ground, Flying, Bug, Rock, Ghost, Steel. Special types: Fire, Water, Grass, Electric, Ice, Psychic, Dragon, Dark. This means Shadow Ball (Ghost) is Physical and Bite (Dark) is Special.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q45
**Q:** How do critical hits work in Gen 2?
**A:** Base critical hit rate is 1/16 (6.25%). High crit ratio moves (Slash, Karate Chop, Razor Leaf) have 1/4 (25%) rate. Critical hits deal 2x damage. Importantly, crits ignore stat stage modifiers — they ignore the defender's positive Defense/SpDef stages AND the attacker's negative Attack/SpAtk stages.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q46
**Q:** How does type effectiveness stack with dual types?
**A:** Type effectiveness multiplies. If a move is super-effective against both types of the defender, it does 4x damage. If super-effective against one and not-very-effective against the other, they cancel to 1x. If super-effective against one and neutral to the other, it's 2x. Two not-very-effectives give 0.25x. An immunity (0x) always overrides everything to 0x.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q47
**Q:** How does the catch rate formula work?
**A:** In Gen 2: `CatchValue = (3 * MaxHP - 2 * CurrentHP) * CatchRate * BallRate / (3 * MaxHP)`. If CatchValue >= 255, automatic catch. Otherwise, 3 shake checks are performed. Lower HP dramatically increases catch rate. Status conditions SHOULD boost catch rate but are bugged in Gen 2 — Sleep/Freeze multiply by 10, Paralysis/Burn/Poison should multiply by 1.5 but the code has a known bug that skips this multiplication.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q48
**Q:** What is the Gen 2 catch rate bug?
**A:** In Gen 2, there is a well-known bug where Paralysis, Burn, and Poison status conditions do NOT actually improve catch rate despite being coded to do so. The status multiplier for these conditions is calculated but never applied to the final catch value. Only Sleep and Freeze correctly boost catch rate (multiplying by 10). This makes Sleep Powder/Spore the best moves for catching Pokemon.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q49
**Q:** How does STAB (Same Type Attack Bonus) work?
**A:** If a Pokemon uses a move that matches one of its types, the damage is multiplied by 1.5x. Example: Typhlosion (Fire) using Flamethrower (Fire) gets 1.5x damage. A dual-type Pokemon only gets STAB once even if the move matches both types.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** beginner

### Q50
**Q:** How does the Speed stat determine turn order?
**A:** The Pokemon with the higher Speed stat attacks first each turn. If Speed is equal, it's a random 50/50 coin flip. Priority moves (Quick Attack, Mach Punch, ExtremeSpeed) always go first regardless of Speed, with their own priority bracket.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** beginner

### Q51
**Q:** How does Paralysis affect a Pokemon?
**A:** Paralysis reduces the Pokemon's Speed stat to 25% of its normal value (75% reduction). Additionally, each turn there is a 25% chance the paralyzed Pokemon is fully paralyzed and cannot move. Paralysis is permanent until cured. Electric-type Pokemon CAN be paralyzed in Gen 2 (immunity was added in Gen 3).
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q52
**Q:** How does Burn work in Gen 2?
**A:** Burn deals 1/8 of maximum HP at the end of each turn and halves the burned Pokemon's Attack stat. Fire-type Pokemon cannot be burned.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q53
**Q:** How does Poison work vs Toxic in Gen 2?
**A:** Regular Poison deals 1/8 max HP per turn. Toxic (badly poisoned) starts at 1/16 max HP and increases by 1/16 each turn (1/16, 2/16, 3/16...). In Gen 2, switching out resets the Toxic counter to 1/16. Steel and Poison types are immune to being poisoned.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q54
**Q:** How does Sleep work in Gen 2?
**A:** Sleep lasts 1-7 turns (randomly determined when inflicted). The sleeping Pokemon cannot take any action except using Sleep Talk or Snore. Rest puts the user to sleep for exactly 2 turns. The turn count decrements at the START of the turn, so a Pokemon that wakes up can immediately attack that turn.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q55
**Q:** How does Freeze work in Gen 2?
**A:** Freeze has a 10% chance to thaw at the start of each turn. Using Flame Wheel or Sacred Fire will thaw the user. Being hit by a Fire-type move also thaws. There is no guaranteed number of turns — a Pokemon can theoretically stay frozen forever. Ice-type Pokemon cannot be frozen.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q56
**Q:** How does Confusion work in Gen 2?
**A:** Confusion lasts 2-5 turns. Each turn, there is a 50% chance the confused Pokemon hits itself instead of executing its chosen move. Self-hit damage uses the formula: `(2 * Level / 5 + 2) * 40 * Attack / Defense / 50 + 2`, treating it as a 40 base power typeless physical attack.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q57
**Q:** How do stat stages work in Gen 2?
**A:** Stats can be modified from -6 to +6 stages. The multipliers are: -6 = 2/8, -5 = 2/7, -4 = 2/6, -3 = 2/5, -2 = 2/4, -1 = 2/3, 0 = 2/2, +1 = 3/2, +2 = 4/2, +3 = 5/2, +4 = 6/2, +5 = 7/2, +6 = 8/2. Each stat (Attack, Defense, Special Attack, Special Defense, Speed, Accuracy, Evasion) has its own stage tracker.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q58
**Q:** How does Belly Drum work?
**A:** Belly Drum cuts the user's current HP by 50% and maximizes the Attack stat to +6 stages. If the user has less than 50% HP, the move fails. It's a high-risk/high-reward move commonly used by Snorlax and Charizard in competitive play.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q59
**Q:** How does Destiny Bond work?
**A:** When a Pokemon uses Destiny Bond, if it faints from an attack before its next turn, the attacking Pokemon also faints. The effect lasts only until the user's next turn. If the user moves again before fainting, the Destiny Bond effect wears off.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q60
**Q:** How does Mean Look work?
**A:** Mean Look prevents the target from switching out or fleeing. The effect lasts until the user switches out or faints. It is commonly combined with Perish Song in competitive play (trap + guaranteed KO after 3 turns).
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q61
**Q:** How does Perish Song work?
**A:** Perish Song affects all Pokemon on the field. After 3 turns, any Pokemon that heard the song and hasn't switched out faints. The counter decrements at the end of each turn (3→2→1→0=faint). Switching out removes the Perish count. Combined with Mean Look/Spider Web to prevent escape.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q62
**Q:** How does Attract work?
**A:** Attract causes the target to become infatuated if it is the opposite gender of the user. An infatuated Pokemon has a 50% chance of not being able to attack each turn. Genderless Pokemon cannot be infatuated. Same-gender Attract always fails.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q63
**Q:** How does Curse work differently for Ghost and non-Ghost types?
**A:** For non-Ghost types: Curse raises Attack and Defense by 1 stage each and lowers Speed by 1 stage. For Ghost types: Curse cuts the user's HP by 50% and places a curse on the target that drains 1/4 of max HP per turn.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q64
**Q:** How does Spikes work in Gen 2?
**A:** Spikes places an entry hazard on the opponent's side. When an opponent Pokemon switches in, it takes damage based on the number of Spikes layers: 1 layer = 1/8 max HP, 2 layers = 1/6 max HP, 3 layers = 1/4 max HP. Flying types and Levitating Pokemon are immune (but Levitate doesn't exist in Gen 2, so only Flying types avoid it). Only Cloyster and Forretress can learn Spikes.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q65
**Q:** How does Rest + Sleep Talk work?
**A:** Rest fully heals HP and puts the user to sleep for 2 turns. Sleep Talk can be used while asleep and randomly selects one of the user's other 3 moves to execute. This combination (RestTalk) allows a Pokemon to heal and continue attacking while asleep. It's a cornerstone strategy in GSC competitive play for Raikou, Zapdos, Snorlax, and others.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q66
**Q:** Does weather exist in Gen 2?
**A:** Yes, but it's limited. Rain Dance boosts Water moves by 50% and reduces Fire moves by 50% for 5 turns. Sunny Day boosts Fire moves by 50% and reduces Water moves by 50% for 5 turns. Sandstorm deals 1/8 max HP damage per turn to non-Rock/Ground/Steel types for 5 turns. There is NO permanent weather (no abilities like Drizzle/Drought in Gen 2).
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q67
**Q:** How does the experience formula work in Gen 2?
**A:** The basic formula is: `EXP = (base_exp * foe_level) / 7`. This is modified by: traded Pokemon get 1.5x EXP, and the Lucky Egg item (not available normally in Crystal) would give 1.5x. EXP is divided among all Pokemon that participated in the battle.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q68
**Q:** How do badge stat boosts work?
**A:** Each badge boosts a specific stat by 12.5% (multiplied by 9/8) in battle. Zephyr Badge: Attack. Plain Badge: Speed. Mineral Badge: Defense. Glacier Badge: Special Attack and Special Defense. These boosts apply to all your Pokemon in battle, not just the one that earned the badge.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q69
**Q:** What is the priority order for moves in Gen 2?
**A:** Priority levels (higher goes first): +2: none in Gen 2 standard. +1: Quick Attack, Mach Punch, ExtremeSpeed. 0: Most moves. -1: Vital Throw, Counter, Mirror Coat. -3: none standard. -5: none standard. -6: none standard. Within the same priority, the faster Pokemon goes first.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q70
**Q:** How does Hidden Power's type and power get determined?
**A:** In Gen 2, Hidden Power's type and power are determined by the Pokemon's DVs (Determinant Values). The type is calculated from the lower 2 bits of Attack, Defense, Speed, and Special DVs. The power ranges from 31 to 70. The formula makes certain type/power combinations impossible. Hidden Power is always Special regardless of its calculated type.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** expert

### Q71
**Q:** How does Encore work?
**A:** Encore forces the target to repeat its last used move for 2-6 turns. The target cannot select a different move or use items. If the target's locked move runs out of PP, it struggles. Encore fails if the target hasn't used a move yet or if the last move was Encore, Mimic, Mirror Move, Sketch, Transform, or Struggle.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q72
**Q:** What happens when a Pokemon is trapped by Spider Web and Baton Pass is used?
**A:** If a Pokemon uses Spider Web to trap the opponent, and then the Spider Web user uses Baton Pass, the trapping effect passes to the incoming Pokemon. The opponent remains unable to switch out. This is a key competitive strategy.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q73
**Q:** How does the Thick Club interact with Marowak?
**A:** The Thick Club item doubles Marowak's Attack stat when held. Combined with Swords Dance (which doubles Attack via +2 stages), Marowak can reach astronomical Attack values. Thick Club only works for Marowak (and Cubone).
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q74
**Q:** How does Protect work? Can it be used consecutively?
**A:** Protect blocks all moves targeting the user for that turn. Using it on consecutive turns halves its success rate each time: 100% → 50% → 25% → 12.5% → etc. This makes it unreliable for stalling.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q75
**Q:** What is the Leftovers + Toxic stall interaction?
**A:** Leftovers heals 1/16 max HP per turn. Toxic deals escalating damage (1/16, 2/16, 3/16...). In the first turn, they cancel out. By turn 2, Toxic outpaces Leftovers. Switching resets the Toxic counter, making Leftovers + switching an effective anti-Toxic strategy.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q76
**Q:** How does Baton Pass work?
**A:** Baton Pass switches the user out and passes all stat stage changes (Attack, Defense, SpA, SpD, Speed boosts/drops), Substitute, Confusion, Curse (ghost), Mean Look/Spider Web trapping, Ingrain, Leech Seed, and Perish Song count to the incoming Pokemon. The incoming Pokemon keeps its own stats and type.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q77
**Q:** Can Electric types be paralyzed in Gen 2?
**A:** Yes. Electric-type Pokemon CAN be paralyzed in Gen 2. The immunity to paralysis for Electric types was not introduced until Generation 6 (X/Y). Thunder Wave and Body Slam can paralyze Electric types.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q78
**Q:** What is the "Gen 2 Berry glitch"?
**A:** In Pokemon Gold and Silver (not Crystal), the internal clock battery dying causes time-based events to stop: berries don't regrow, daily events don't reset, and happiness evolution becomes impossible. Crystal partially fixed this. This isn't a gameplay glitch but a hardware limitation.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q79
**Q:** How does the Focus Band work?
**A:** The Focus Band has a ~11.7% chance (approximately 30/256) of preventing the holder from fainting from an attack that would otherwise KO it, leaving it at 1 HP instead. It can activate multiple times per battle.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q80
**Q:** How does Thief work in Gen 2?
**A:** Thief is a 40 base power Dark-type move. If the user is not holding an item and the target is holding an item, the user steals the target's held item. In Gen 2, Thief is Special (Dark type = Special). The stolen item is kept permanently after battle.
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q81
**Q:** How does Counter work?
**A:** Counter reflects Physical damage dealt to the user on the same turn at 2x power. It has -1 priority (always goes last in its priority bracket). In Gen 2, Counter only works against Physical moves (Normal, Fighting, Poison, Ground, Flying, Bug, Rock, Ghost, Steel typed moves).
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q82
**Q:** How does Mirror Coat work?
**A:** Mirror Coat reflects Special damage dealt to the user on the same turn at 2x power. It has -1 priority. In Gen 2, Mirror Coat works against Special moves (Fire, Water, Grass, Electric, Ice, Psychic, Dragon, Dark typed moves).
**Category:** mechanics **Difficulty:** advanced

## Section 3: Strategic Questions (Team Building, Gym Strategy, Competitive)

### Q83
**Q:** What is the best starter for speedrunning Pokemon Crystal?
**A:** Totodile. Feraligatr has the best combination of physical stats, STAB Surf, and wide coverage moves (Ice Punch, ThunderPunch from Goldenrod TMs). It can sweep most of the game solo.
**Category:** strategy **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q84
**Q:** How do I beat Whitney's Miltank?
**A:** Key strategies: 1) Use a Female Pokemon (immune to Attract). 2) Trade Drowzee for Machop in Goldenrod Dept Store (Fighting-type, female). 3) Use Geodude/Graveler (resists Rollout). 4) Status moves (Paralysis, Sleep) interrupt Rollout's chain. 5) Don't let Rollout build up — if it hits 3+ times, it can OHKO.
**Category:** strategy **Difficulty:** beginner

### Q85
**Q:** What is the best team for the Elite Four?
**A:** A strong team includes: 1) Ice Beam user (Lapras, Jynx — for Lance's Dragonite), 2) Electric user (Ampharos, Raikou — for Gyarados/Charizard), 3) Psychic user (Alakazam, Espeon — for Koga/Bruno), 4) Fighting user (Machamp, Heracross — for Karen's Umbreon/Houndoom), 5-6) Your starter + flexible coverage. Recommended level: 45-48.
**Category:** strategy **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q86
**Q:** How do I catch the roaming legendaries (Raikou/Entei)?
**A:** Steps: 1) Encounter them in Johto routes (check Pokedex map after first encounter). 2) Use a fast Pokemon with Mean Look (Crobat, Gengar) to prevent fleeing. 3) Damage carries over between encounters, so chip damage works. 4) Status them with Sleep/Freeze. 5) Use the Master Ball if you don't want to bother. 6) Fast Ball from White Apricorn has a bonus against fleeing Pokemon.
**Category:** strategy **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q87
**Q:** What Pokemon should I teach Surf to?
**A:** Best Surf users: Feraligatr/Croconaw (STAB + strong SpA), Red Gyarados (though uses Attack, not SpA), Lapras (STAB + tank), Quagsire (Water/Ground is great defensive typing). Avoid teaching Surf to a Pokemon that doesn't benefit from it — it's a permanent HM move in Gen 2.
**Category:** strategy **Difficulty:** beginner

### Q88
**Q:** What is the optimal order to fight Kanto gym leaders?
**A:** The most efficient order is: 1) Lt. Surge (immediate), 2) Sabrina (adjacent), 3) Erika (adjacent), 4) Janine (Fuchsia), 5) Brock (after Power Plant quest), 6) Misty (requires Power Plant quest), 7) Blaine (Seafoam, needs Surf), 8) Blue (last). However, the order is flexible — you can challenge them in any order.
**Category:** strategy **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q89
**Q:** Why is Snorlax considered the best Pokemon in GSC competitive?

**A:** Spikes is arguably the most important move in GSC competitive. Only Cloyster and Forretress learn it. Key users: Misdreavus (Mean Look + Perish Song + Protect) and Gengar (Mean Look + Perish Song + Hypnosis).

## Section 4: Edge Case Questions (Glitches, Obscure Interactions)

### Q103
**Q:** Can you encounter Celebi in Pokemon Crystal without events?
**A:** In the original Japanese Crystal, you need the GS Ball event item. In the Virtual Console (3DS) release of Pokemon Crystal, the GS Ball is automatically available after entering the Hall of Fame, allowing you to encounter Celebi at the Ilex Forest Shrine at Lv30. There is no legitimate way to get Celebi in Gold/Silver.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q104
**Q:** What happens if all 3 Dragonair on Clair's team use Thunder Wave?
**A:** You get paralyzed by the first one. Subsequent Thunder Waves will fail because a Pokemon can only have one status condition at a time (Paralysis, Burn, Poison, Sleep, Freeze are all primary status — they don't stack). Confusion IS separate and can stack with a primary status.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q105
**Q:** Can you get all 3 starters in a single playthrough?
**A:** Not without trading. You receive 1 starter from Elm. To get all 3, you need two friends with the game (or two additional cartridges) to trade the other starters to you. There is no in-game method to obtain the other 2 starters.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** beginner

### Q106
**Q:** What happens if you KO the Red Gyarados instead of catching it?
**A:** Nothing bad happens. You still get the Red Scale (it's on the ground), you still meet Lance, and the Rocket Hideout subplot proceeds normally. The Red Gyarados is gone forever, though — it doesn't respawn.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** beginner

### Q107
**Q:** Can Ditto transform into a shiny Pokemon and be shiny?
**A:** In Gen 2, shininess is determined by DVs (IVs). When Ditto transforms, it copies the target's appearance but keeps its own DVs. So a non-shiny Ditto transforming into a shiny Pokemon will NOT appear shiny. Conversely, a shiny Ditto transforming into a regular Pokemon will appear shiny in the transformed state.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** expert

### Q108
**Q:** What happens if you use Metronome and it selects a move with 0 PP?
**A:** Metronome randomly selects any move (except itself, Struggle, Sketch, Mimic, and a few others). PP is not checked for the selected move — it always works regardless of the user's PP situation. The selected move executes normally.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q109
**Q:** Can you fish for Lapras in Union Cave outside of Friday?
**A:** No. Lapras appears ONLY on Fridays in Union Cave B2F. It appears as an overworld sprite swimming in the water, not as a fishing encounter. If you KO or catch it, it reappears the following Friday. It's always Lv20.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q110
**Q:** What determines if a Pokemon is shiny in Gen 2?
**A:** A Pokemon is shiny if its DVs meet specific criteria: Attack DV must be 2, 3, 6, 7, 10, 11, 14, or 15. Defense, Speed, and Special DVs must all be 10. HP DV is calculated from the others and is always 0 or 8 for shinies. The probability is approximately 1/8192.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** expert

### Q111
**Q:** Does Explosion hit Ghost types in Gen 2?
**A:** No. Explosion is a Normal-type move, and Ghost types are immune to Normal. This is a common mistake — some players forget because Explosion is "an explosion" that seems like it should hit everything.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q112
**Q:** What happens if Wobbuffet fights another Wobbuffet?
**A:** This creates an infinite battle scenario. Wobbuffet only knows Counter, Mirror Coat, Safeguard, and Destiny Bond — all reactive moves. Neither Wobbuffet can deal damage first, so the battle stalls. In competitive play, this is handled by the timer. Shadow Tag (Wobbuffet's ability) doesn't exist in Gen 2, so either player can switch out.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q113
**Q:** Can you get Eevee before Ecruteak City?
**A:** No. Bill's Eevee (Lv20) requires meeting Bill first, which happens in Ecruteak City. After that, Eevee is available at Bill's house in Goldenrod City. There are no other ways to obtain Eevee in Crystal without trading.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** beginner

### Q114
**Q:** What happens if you lose to Trainer Red?
**A:** You black out and return to the last Pokemon Center. You can challenge Red again anytime by returning to the summit of Mt. Silver. There is no penalty beyond losing half your money (same as any other loss).
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** beginner

### Q115
**Q:** Can the daycare Pokemon learn new moves while leveling up?
**A:** Yes. Pokemon in the Day Care gain 1 EXP for every step you take. When they reach a level-up threshold, they can learn moves. However, they automatically delete old moves to make room for new ones (starting from the top of the move list), which can cause them to lose important moves without your consent.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q116
**Q:** Does switching out reset stat changes in Gen 2?
**A:** Yes. Switching a Pokemon out and back in resets ALL stat stage changes (Attack, Defense, SpA, SpD, Speed, Accuracy, Evasion) to 0. It also removes Confusion, Encore, and Leech Seed. However, it does NOT remove primary status conditions (Paralysis, Burn, Poison, Sleep, Freeze) — those persist until cured.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q117
**Q:** What is the Coin Case glitch?
**A:** In Pokemon Gold/Silver (not Crystal), viewing the Coin Case while certain audio is playing triggers a buffer overflow that allows arbitrary code execution. Speedrunners exploit this to warp directly to the credits or manipulate game state. This was patched in Pokemon Crystal.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** expert

### Q118
**Q:** Can you breed legendary Pokemon?
**A:** No. Legendary Pokemon (Raikou, Entei, Suicune, Lugia, Ho-Oh, Celebi) and most mythical Pokemon cannot breed. They are in the "Undiscovered" egg group. The only exceptions are Manaphy (not in Gen 2) and Ditto breeding (Ditto can breed with anything except other Dittos and Undiscovered group).
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q119
**Q:** What happens if you have no room for a Pokemon you must receive?
**A:** If your party is full and PC boxes are full, the game typically won't give you the Pokemon until you make room. For story-critical Pokemon (starters), the game ensures you have space. For gift Pokemon like Eevee, you'll be told you need room first.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q120
**Q:** Can Focus Band activate against a critical hit?
**A:** Yes. Focus Band checks after damage is applied. If the damage (even from a critical hit) would KO the Pokemon, Focus Band has its ~11.7% chance to activate and leave the holder at 1 HP.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q121
**Q:** What happens if you use Thief on a trainer's Pokemon?
**A:** In Gen 2 trainer battles, Thief steals the item for the duration of the battle, but the item is NOT permanently stolen from the trainer. You lose the stolen item after the battle ends. In wild battles, you keep the stolen item permanently.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q122
**Q:** Can a frozen Pokemon be put to sleep?
**A:** No. A Pokemon can only have one primary status condition at a time. If a Pokemon is frozen, Sleep Powder will fail. Similarly, if a Pokemon is paralyzed, Toxic will fail. The five primary statuses (Burn, Freeze, Paralysis, Poison, Sleep) are mutually exclusive.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q123
**Q:** What is the maximum damage possible in a single hit in Gen 2?
**A:** The theoretical maximum involves: Level 100 Shuckle with max Attack DV + stat experience, holding Thick Club (doesn't work on Shuckle, so use Power Trick... which doesn't exist in Gen 2). Realistically, the highest practical damage comes from a Level 100 Marowak with Thick Club using Earthquake on a 4x-weak target after max Swords Dance. This can deal over 1000 damage.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** expert

### Q124
**Q:** Are abilities present in Gen 2?
**A:** No. Abilities were introduced in Generation 3 (Ruby/Sapphire). In Gen 2, Pokemon have no abilities. This means: no Intimidate, no Levitate, no Flash Fire, no Water Absorb, etc. This fundamentally changes competitive dynamics compared to later generations.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** beginner

### Q125
**Q:** Are natures present in Gen 2?
**A:** No. Natures were introduced in Generation 3. In Gen 2, all Pokemon of the same species with the same DVs and stat experience have identical stats. There are no stat-modifying natures.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** beginner

### Q126
**Q:** What is the difference between DVs and IVs?
**A:** DVs (Determinant Values, used in Gen 1-2) range from 0-15 for each stat. IVs (Individual Values, Gen 3+) range from 0-31. DVs determine stats differently: Attack, Defense, Speed, and Special have separate DVs; HP DV is derived from the others. Gender and shininess are also determined by DVs in Gen 2.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q127
**Q:** Can you use Fly to reach Cianwood City?
**A:** Only after you've visited Cianwood City at least once. Fly requires having previously visited the destination. You must Surf from Olivine through Routes 40-41 the first time.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** beginner

### Q128
**Q:** What happens to the legendary beasts if you KO them?
**A:** If you KO Raikou, Entei, or Suicune (roaming), they are gone forever. They do not respawn. This is why saving before encountering them is critical. In Crystal, Suicune at Tin Tower also does not respawn if KO'd.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q129
**Q:** Can wild Ditto be shiny in Gen 2?
**A:** Yes, but when Ditto transforms, it copies the opposing Pokemon's appearance including color palette. So even if you encounter a shiny Ditto, it will transform and look like the opposing Pokemon's normal or shiny form depending on the opponent's DVs. The Ditto's own DVs determine if IT is shiny.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** expert

### Q130
**Q:** What is the Cute Charm glitch in Gen 2?
**A:** This isn't a Gen 2 thing. The Cute Charm glitch exists in Gen 4 (Diamond/Pearl/Platinum) where Cute Charm can force encountered Pokemon to have specific DVs, dramatically increasing shiny rates. Gen 2 has no abilities, so no Cute Charm.
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q131
**Q:** Can you rematch gym leaders in Pokemon Crystal?
**A:** Not directly. However, you can rematch certain trainers via the Pokegear phone system. Specific trainers registered in your phone will call for rematches on certain days. Gym leaders themselves cannot be rematched in Gen 2 (rematches were introduced in HGSS).
**Category:** edge_case **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q132
**Q:** What Pokemon in Gen 2 can learn the most moves?
**A:** Mew can learn every TM and HM in the game, making it the most versatile (but it's banned to Ubers). Among non-legendary Pokemon, Snorlax and Dragonite have extremely wide movepools.

## Section 6: Meta Questions (Design, Comparisons, Historical)

### Q156
**Q:** What are the main differences between Gen 2 and Gen 3 mechanics?
**A:** Major changes: 1) Abilities introduced (Intimidate, Levitate, etc.). 2) Natures introduced (stat-modifying personalities). 3) EVs replaced stat experience (max 510 total, 252 per stat). 4) IVs replaced DVs (0-31 range instead of 0-15). 5) Physical/Special determined per MOVE instead of per TYPE. 6) Double battles introduced. 7) Fairy type didn't exist until Gen 6.
**Category:** meta **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q157
**Q:** Why is the physical/special split by type considered a design flaw?
**A:** Because many Pokemon have mismatched stats and typing. Examples: Gyarados has 125 Attack but Water (Special) STAB — its Water moves use its weaker Special Attack. Sneasel has 95 Attack but Dark (Special) and Ice (Special) — it can't use STAB effectively. Flareon has 130 Attack but Fire (Special). This was fixed in Gen 4 with per-move physical/special categorization.
**Category:** meta **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q158
**Q:** Why is Snorlax not banned in GSC despite being so dominant?
**A:** Although Snorlax has ~95% usage in competitive GSC, it has sufficient counterplay: Machamp, Heracross, Skarmory, and Tyranitar all handle it. The metagame has adapted around Snorlax — teams are built to handle it. Banning Snorlax would fundamentally change the tier and isn't considered necessary because the game is still strategically diverse.
**Category:** meta **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q159
**Q:** How does Pokemon Crystal differ from Gold/Silver?
**A:** Crystal-specific features: 1) Animated Pokemon sprites. 2) Play as a girl (Kris, first female protagonist). 3) Battle Tower in Olivine City. 4) Suicune has a fixed encounter storyline (not roaming). 5) Move Tutor in Goldenrod Game Corner (Ice Beam, Thunderbolt, Flamethrower). 6) Bug Catching Contest has wider Pokemon pool. 7) Odd Egg from Day Care (good DVs, 14% shiny chance). 8) Celebi event with GS Ball (Japanese version).
**Category:** meta **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q160
**Q:** What makes Gen 2 competitive unique compared to modern Pokemon?
**A:** Key unique aspects: 1) No abilities — makes certain Pokemon worse/better than in later gens. 2) Leftovers on everything — no choice items (Choice Band came Gen 3). 3) Spikes is the ONLY entry hazard. 4) Small viable Pokemon pool (~25 OU Pokemon). 5) Games are long and defensive. 6) No team preview — surprise factor matters. 7) Explosion is much more viable (higher base power relative to Gen 5+). 8) Baton Pass chains are legal and viable.
**Category:** meta **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q161
**Q:** Why was Celebi banned to Ubers?
**A:** Celebi's typing (Grass/Psychic) combined with its 100 base stats across the board, plus its movepool (Heal Bell, Recover, Leech Seed, Baton Pass, Perish Song, Future Sight) makes it too versatile and bulky. It can cleric, stall, pass stats, and attack. Its only event distribution (Japanese Crystal GS Ball) made it extremely rare, but its stats justified the ban regardless.
**Category:** meta **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q162
**Q:** What design philosophy changes happened between Gen 1 and Gen 2?
**A:** Major shifts: 1) Special stat split into SpAtk/SpDef (Gen 1 had one "Special"). 2) Dark and Steel types added (to balance Psychic dominance from Gen 1). 3) Held items introduced. 4) Breeding introduced. 5) Day/night cycle added. 6) Friendship/happiness system. 7) Shiny Pokemon concept. 8) Two regions in one game. 9) Phone system for trainer rematches. 10) More balanced type chart.
**Category:** meta **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q163
**Q:** What is the significance of the Red battle at Mt. Silver?
**A:** Trainer Red represents the player character from Gen 1 (Red/Blue/Yellow). His team reflects the canonical Gen 1 party: Pikachu (starter in Yellow), the three fully-evolved Kanto starters, plus Espeon and Snorlax. At Lv81 Pikachu and Lv77 others, he is the highest-level trainer in any Pokemon game up to that point. The battle serves as the true final challenge and connects Gen 1 to Gen 2 narratively.
**Category:** meta **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q164
**Q:** Why does Gen 2 have two regions while later games have only one?
**A:** Game Freak originally planned for Gold/Silver to be the final Pokemon games. They wanted to create a definitive experience that concluded the series, so they included both Johto (new) and Kanto (nostalgia). This was possible partly because the Game Boy Color had limited graphics that took less storage space. Later generations had more complex graphics and features that consumed the available ROM/cartridge space, making dual regions impractical.
**Category:** meta **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q165
**Q:** How balanced is Gen 2 compared to Gen 1?
**A:** Gen 2 is significantly more balanced than Gen 1. Key improvements: 1) Psychic type no longer dominates (Dark type added as counter; Bug and Ghost moves buffed). 2) Special split into SpAtk/SpDef prevents single-stat dominance. 3) Steel type provides defensive balance. 4) More viable competitive Pokemon. 5) Fewer glitches/exploits. 6) Items add strategic depth. Gen 2 still has issues (type-based physical/special split, Snorlax centralization) but is much healthier than Gen 1's Mewtwo/Alakazam meta.
**Category:** meta **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q166
**Q:** What Pokemon were added in Gen 2?
**A:** Gen 2 added 100 new Pokemon (#152-#251): the Johto starters (Chikorita line, Cyndaquil line, Totodile line), pre-evolutions of Gen 1 Pokemon (Pichu, Cleffa, Igglybuff, etc.), new evolutions of Gen 1 Pokemon (Crobat, Espeon, Umbreon, Steelix, Scizor, Kingdra, etc.), legendary beasts (Raikou, Entei, Suicune), tower legends (Lugia, Ho-Oh), and Celebi.
**Category:** meta **Difficulty:** beginner

### Q167
**Q:** What are the Gen 2-specific Pokemon types that were added?
**A:** Gen 2 added two new types: Dark and Steel. Dark was added specifically to counter Psychic (which dominated Gen 1). Dark is immune to Psychic and super-effective against it. Steel was added for defensive balance — it resists 11 types. Notable Dark types: Umbreon, Murkrow, Houndoom, Sneasel, Tyranitar. Notable Steel types: Steelix, Skarmory, Scizor, Forretress, Magneton (retroactively gained Steel).
**Category:** meta **Difficulty:** beginner

### Q168
**Q:** What key items from Gen 2 became staples in future generations?
**A:** Key innovations: 1) Held items (carried into all future gens). 2) Breeding system (core mechanic ever since). 3) Shiny Pokemon (became a massive collecting metagame). 4) Day/night system (expanded in every gen). 5) Happiness evolution (became more prevalent). 6) Berries (expanded into Berry farming in Gen 3+). 7) TMs being reusable (introduced in Gen 5, but TMs started here).
**Category:** meta **Difficulty:** intermediate

### Q169
**Q:** Why did the competitive community choose Smogon rules over VGC for Gen 2?
**A:** VGC (Video Game Championships) didn't exist until Gen 4 (2009). Gen 2 competitive was entirely fan-organized, primarily through Smogon University, Azure Heights, and GameFAQs. Smogon's tier system (Ubers/OU/UU) and clauses (Sleep Clause, Species Clause, Evasion Clause) became the de facto standard because they created the most balanced and diverse metagame.
**Category:** meta **Difficulty:** advanced

### Q170
**Q:** What is the "Special split" and why did it matter?
**A:** In Gen 1, there was a single "Special" stat that governed both Special offense and Special defense. In Gen 2, this was split into Special Attack and Special Defense. This massively changed balance: Pokemon like Chansey (high Gen 1 Special) got high SpDef but low SpAtk. Alakazam kept high SpAtk but got lower SpDef. The split made more Pokemon competitively viable by differentiating offensive and defensive special capabilities.
**Category:** meta **Difficulty:** intermediate

**Q200:** What is Smeargle's signature move? **A:** Sketch. It permanently copies the last move used by the opponent. Smeargle is the only Pokemon that learns Sketch. This allows Smeargle to theoretically know any move in the game. **Cat:** moves

**Q201:** What is the Bug-Catching Contest and what are the prizes? **A:** Held Tue/Thu/Sat in National Park. You get 1 Pokemon + 20 Sport Balls, 20-minute time limit. **Q202:** How many TMs are there in Gen 2? **A:** 50 TMs (TM01-TM50) and 7 HMs (HM01-HM07). TMs are single-use in Gen 2 (they became reusable in Gen 5). **Cat:** items

**Q203:** What is the fastest way to increase friendship/happiness? **A:** Walk 256 steps (+1), level up (+2-5 depending on current happiness), vitamins (+2-5), Haircut Brothers in Goldenrod Underground (+1-5, daily). Friendship evolves at 220+ happiness. Trading resets friendship to base. **Cat:** mechanics

**Q204:** What determines gender in Gen 2? **A:** Gender is determined by the Attack DV. If the Attack DV is below the species' gender threshold, the Pokemon is female; otherwise male. This means female Pokemon always have lower Attack DVs, making them slightly weaker physically than males of the same species. **Cat:** mechanics

**Q205:** Can you soft-reset for shinies in Gen 2? **A:** Yes. Shininess is determined at encounter/generation time based on DVs. You can save before encountering a legendary/gift Pokemon and soft-reset (A+B+Start+Select) until it's shiny. The probability is ~1/8192 per attempt. **Cat:** mechanics
