# Pokemon Crystal - Comprehensive FAQ

100+ entries covering the most commonly asked questions about Pokemon Crystal. Designed for vector DB retrieval -- each Q&A pair is self-contained.

## Getting Specific Pokemon

### Q: How do I get Eevee in Pokemon Crystal?
**A:** Bill gives you an Eevee in Goldenrod City. Visit Bill's house (located near the Pokemon Center) after meeting him at Ecruteak City's Pokemon Center. The Eevee is Level 20. You can only get one per save file. Evolve it with stones (Water Stone = Vaporeon, Thunder Stone = Jolteon, Fire Stone = Flareon) or friendship (day = Espeon, night = Umbreon).

### Q: How do I get Tyrogue in Pokemon Crystal?
**A:** Defeat Blackbelt Kiyo in Mt. Mortar's innermost cave (requires Surf and Waterfall). He gives you a Level 10 Tyrogue. It evolves at Level 20 into Hitmonlee (Attack > Defense), Hitmonchan (Defense > Attack), or Hitmontop (Attack = Defense).

### Q: How do I get Lapras in Pokemon Crystal?
**A:** Lapras appears every Friday in the basement of Union Cave (B2F). You need Surf to reach it. It is Level 20 and only appears on Fridays. If you faint it, it returns next Friday.

### Q: How do I get all three starters?
**A:** You pick one (Cyndaquil, Totodile, or Chikorita) from Prof. Elm. The only way to get all three in a single game is through trading. There is no in-game event that gives you the other two.

### Q: How do I catch Suicune in Pokemon Crystal?
**A:** In Crystal (unlike Gold/Silver), Suicune has a fixed encounter. After encountering it at Cianwood City, Mahogany Town (Route 42 entrance), and Mt. Mortar, it waits for you in the Tin Tower Basement (accessible from the Burned Tower). It is Level 40. You need the Clear Bell (obtained from the Ecruteak Dance Theater after beating the Wise Trio) to access it.

### Q: How do I catch Ho-Oh in Pokemon Crystal?
**A:** After defeating the Elite Four, obtain the Rainbow Wing from the director of the Goldenrod Radio Tower (he gives it to you after the Rocket event). Go to Tin Tower in Ecruteak City. You also need the Clear Bell. Climb to the roof. Ho-Oh is Level 60.

### Q: How do I catch Lugia in Pokemon Crystal?
**A:** After defeating the Elite Four, obtain the Silver Wing from an old man in Pewter City (Kanto). Go to the Whirl Islands (between Olivine and Cianwood, need Whirlpool and Surf). Navigate to the bottom floor. Lugia is Level 60. You also need the Tidal Bell from the Ecruteak Dance Theater.

### Q: How do I catch Celebi in Pokemon Crystal?
**A:** Celebi is only available through the GS Ball event, which was originally Japan-only for Mobile System GB. In the Virtual Console release, the GS Ball event is activated after entering the Hall of Fame. Kurt will take the GS Ball, and you can encounter Celebi in the Ilex Forest shrine at Level 30.

### Q: How do I get Dratini in Pokemon Crystal?
**A:** Three ways: (1) Dragon's Den -- answer the Shrine Elder's questions correctly and he gives you a Dratini with ExtremeSpeed (if all answers are "good"). (2) Fish in Dragon's Den with a Good Rod or Super Rod. (3) Game Corner prize in Goldenrod (2100 coins).

### Q: Where do I find Larvitar?
**A:** Mt. Silver exterior only. Very rare encounter (about 5% on certain floors). Levels 15-20. Not available until post-game since Mt. Silver requires all 16 badges.

### Q: How do I get Snorlax?
**A:** There is no wild Snorlax in Crystal. You must trade from Red/Blue/Yellow or Gold/Silver. The sleeping Snorlax from RBY does not appear in GSC.

### Q: Where can I find Chansey?
**A:** Route 13, 14, and 15 in Kanto during the morning or day. Very rare (about 1% encounter rate).

### Q: How do I get Togepi?
**A:** The Mystery Egg from Mr. Pokemon on Route 30 (given to Prof. Elm, then Elm's aide returns it). Hatch it by walking approximately 5120 steps. It hatches into Togepi.

### Q: How do I find Unown?
**A:** Ruins of Alph. Solve the sliding puzzles in the four chambers (Ho-Oh, Kabuto, Omanyte, Aerodactyl). Each solved puzzle opens the floor to the Inner Chamber where Unown appear. There are 26 forms (A-Z). After catching 3+ forms, the Unown Dex is enabled.

### Q: Where do I find Heracross?
**A:** Headbutt trees. Found on Routes 29, 30, 31, 32, 33, 42, 43, 44, 45, 46 and in Azalea Town, National Park, and other areas. Only certain trees yield Heracross -- each tree has a fixed encounter table determined by your Trainer ID.

### Q: How do I get Umbreon?
**A:** Evolve Eevee with high friendship during nighttime (6:00 PM - 3:59 AM). Friendship is raised by walking, leveling up, using vitamins, and getting haircuts in Goldenrod Underground. Eevee needs to level up at night with 220+ friendship to evolve.

### Q: How do I get Espeon?
**A:** Same as Umbreon but during daytime (4:00 AM - 5:59 PM). Evolve Eevee with 220+ friendship by leveling up during the day.

### Q: Where do I find Sneasel?
**A:** Route 28 and Mt. Silver (exterior) at night only. Also found in Ice Path at very low encounter rates.

### Q: Where do I find Skarmory?
**A:** Route 45 only. About 5% encounter rate during morning/day. Not available at night.

### Q: Where do I find Phanpy?
**A:** Route 45 only, during the morning (4:00-9:59 AM). About 5% encounter rate. Evolves into Donphan at Level 25.

### Q: Where do I find Teddiursa?
**A:** Route 45 during morning only. About 5% encounter rate. Evolves into Ursaring at Level 30.

### Q: How do I catch Entei and Raikou?
**A:** After releasing the Legendary Beasts from the Burned Tower basement (first visit with Eusine), Entei and Raikou roam randomly across Johto routes. Check the Map Card on your Pokegear to track them (after encountering once). They flee on turn 1 unless you use Mean Look/Spider Web or a trapping move. Their HP and status persist between encounters. Use a Fast Ball or Master Ball.

## Evolution Questions

### Q: How do I evolve Onix into Steelix?
**A:** Trade Onix while it holds a Metal Coat. Metal Coat can be found on the S.S. Aqua (or held by wild Magnemite at about 8% chance).

### Q: How do I evolve Scyther into Scizor?
**A:** Trade Scyther while it holds a Metal Coat. Same item needed as Steelix.

### Q: How do I evolve Slowpoke into Slowking?
**A:** Trade Slowpoke while it holds a King's Rock. King's Rock can be found in Slowpoke Well or held by wild Poliwhirl (8% chance).

### Q: How do I evolve Seadra into Kingdra?
**A:** Trade Seadra while it holds a Dragon Scale. Dragon Scale can be found in Mt. Mortar or held by wild Horsea/Seadra (8% chance).

### Q: How do I evolve Poliwhirl into Politoed?
**A:** Trade Poliwhirl while it holds a King's Rock.

### Q: How do I evolve Porygon into Porygon2?
**A:** Trade Porygon while it holds an Up-Grade. The Up-Grade is given by the Silph Co. representative in Saffron City (Kanto).

### Q: How do I evolve Golbat into Crobat?
**A:** Level up Golbat with high friendship (220+). Friendship is gained through walking, leveling up, haircuts, and vitamins.

### Q: How does Tyrogue's evolution work?
**A:** At Level 20: Attack > Defense = Hitmonlee. Defense > Attack = Hitmonchan. Attack = Defense = Hitmontop. Use vitamins (Protein/Iron) or Rare Candies to manipulate stats before reaching Level 20.

### Q: How do I evolve Chansey into Blissey?
**A:** Level up Chansey with high friendship (220+).

### Q: How do I evolve Eevee? What are all the options?
**A:** Five evolutions: Water Stone = Vaporeon, Thunder Stone = Jolteon, Fire Stone = Flareon, Friendship (day level-up) = Espeon, Friendship (night level-up) = Umbreon. Friendship must be 220+ for Espeon/Umbreon.

### Q: What Pokemon evolve by trading?
**A:** Plain trade: Kadabra (Alakazam), Machoke (Machamp), Graveler (Golem), Haunter (Gengar). Trade with item: Onix+Metal Coat (Steelix), Scyther+Metal Coat (Scizor), Slowpoke+King's Rock (Slowking), Poliwhirl+King's Rock (Politoed), Seadra+Dragon Scale (Kingdra), Porygon+Up-Grade (Porygon2).

### Q: What Pokemon evolve by friendship?
**A:** Golbat (Crobat), Chansey (Blissey), Eevee (Espeon during day, Umbreon at night), Pichu (Pikachu), Cleffa (Clefairy), Igglybuff (Jigglypuff), Togepi (Togetic).

### Q: What Pokemon evolve with stones?
**A:** Water Stone: Poliwhirl (Poliwrath), Shellder (Cloyster), Staryu (Starmie), Eevee (Vaporeon). Thunder Stone: Pikachu (Raichu), Eevee (Jolteon). Fire Stone: Vulpix (Ninetales), Growlithe (Arcanine), Eevee (Flareon). Moon Stone: Nidorina (Nidoqueen), Nidorino (Nidoking), Clefairy (Clefable), Jigglypuff (Wigglytuff). Leaf Stone: Gloom (Vileplume), Weepinbell (Victreebel), Exeggcute (Exeggutor). Sun Stone: Gloom (Bellossom), Sunkern (Sunflora).

**A:** Lance gives it to you in the Team Rocket HQ basement in Mahogany Town after defeating the Electrode. You need the Glacier Badge (from Pryce) to use it.

**A:** Ilex Forest. Help the Charcoal Maker's apprentice retrieve the Farfetch'd. He gives you HM01 (Cut). You need the Hive Badge (from Bugsy) to use it.

**A:** Mt. Mortar (deep interior, requires Surf and Waterfall). Also held by wild Horsea and Seadra (8% chance, fish in Whirl Islands).

**A:** Slowpoke Well (B1F, hidden item). Also held by wild Poliwhirl (8% chance).

**A:** Prof. Elm gives it to you after defeating Team Rocket at the Goldenrod Radio Tower. He calls you on the phone, then you pick it up at Elm's Lab in New Bark Town.

### Q: Where is the Exp. Share?
**A:** Mr. Pokemon on Route 30 gives it to you when you show him a Red Scale (obtained from the shiny Gyarados at Lake of Rage).

### Q: Where is the Coin Case?
**A:** Goldenrod City Underground. Talk to the clown (Mr. Game) in the tunnel. He gives you the Coin Case for free.

### Q: Where do I get the Good Rod?
**A:** Olivine City. A fisherman in a house near the port gives it to you.

### Q: Where do I get the Super Rod?
**A:** Route 12 in Kanto. A fisherman's house south of Lavender Town.

### Q: Where do I get the Old Rod?
**A:** Route 32. A fisherman near the Pokemon Center gives it to you.

## Game Mechanics

### Q: How does the day/night cycle work?
**A:** The game uses a real-time clock. Morning is 4:00-9:59 AM, Day is 10:00 AM-5:59 PM, Night is 6:00 PM-3:59 AM. Time affects wild encounters (certain Pokemon appear only at specific times), NPC availability, some events (Lapras on Fridays), and radio programming. The clock is set at game start and runs even when the system is off.

### Q: How do DVs work in Gen 2? Are they the same as IVs?
**A:** Gen 2 uses DVs (Determinant Values), not IVs. Each stat (Attack, Defense, Speed, Special) has a DV from 0-15. HP DV is calculated from the other four DVs. DVs are half the range of modern IVs (0-31). The Special DV controls both Special Attack and Special Defense. A Pokemon's gender is determined by its Attack DV (female if Attack DV <= gender threshold). Shiny status requires: Attack DV = 2, 3, 6, 7, 10, 11, 14, or 15; Defense DV = 10; Speed DV = 10; Special DV = 10.

### Q: How does the Physical/Special split work in Gen 2?
**A:** There is NO individual move physical/special flag. All moves of a given type use the same category: Physical types: Normal, Fighting, Poison, Ground, Flying, Bug, Rock, Ghost, Steel. Special types: Fire, Water, Grass, Electric, Ice, Psychic, Dragon, Dark. This means Gyarados's Water moves use Special Attack (despite Gyarados having higher Attack), and Alakazam's Elemental Punches use Attack stat (since Fighting is physical).

### Q: How does breeding work?
**A:** Leave two compatible Pokemon at the Day Care on Route 34. Pokemon are compatible if they share an Egg Group and are opposite genders (or one is Ditto). The offspring is always the mother's species (lowest evolution). Egg Moves are inherited from the father. DVs are partially inherited (Defense DV from father, Special DV from mother, with some randomization). Eggs hatch after a species-specific step count.

### Q: How do I check friendship?
**A:** Visit the Happiness Rater in Goldenrod City (house near the Bike Shop). She gives a vague description of friendship level. Also, the haircut brothers in Goldenrod Underground raise friendship. Friendship caps at 255. Most friendship evolutions require 220+.

### Q: How does the shiny system work in Gen 2?
**A:** Shininess is determined by DVs. A Pokemon is shiny if: Attack DV is 2, 3, 6, 7, 10, 11, 14, or 15; AND Defense, Speed, and Special DVs are all exactly 10. The probability is 1/8192. Breeding with a shiny parent gives improved odds in Gen 2 due to DV inheritance.

### Q: How do held items work?
**A:** Pokemon can hold one item in battle. Effects include: Berries (auto-heal status), type-boosting items (10% boost like Charcoal for Fire), Leftovers (1/16 HP per turn), Focus Band (12% chance to survive fatal hit at 1 HP), King's Rock (10% flinch on damaging moves), Quick Claw (23.4% chance to go first). Items are consumed when used (berries, Focus Band) or permanent (Leftovers, type boosters).

### Q: What is the stat experience (Stat Exp) system?
**A:** Gen 2 uses Stat Experience, not EVs. Every stat can accumulate up to 65535 stat experience points. At Level 100, stat exp. contributes sqrt(stat_exp)/4 to the stat. There is no total cap (unlike modern 510 EV cap), so a Pokemon can max all stats. Stat experience is gained equal to the defeated Pokemon's base stats. Vitamins add 2560 stat exp per use, maxing at 25600 per vitamin type.

### Q: How does the type chart work in Gen 2? Are there new types?
**A:** Gen 2 added Steel and Dark types. Key matchups: Steel resists many types (Normal, Flying, Rock, Bug, Steel, Grass, Psychic, Ice, Dragon) but is weak to Fire, Fighting, Ground. Dark is immune to Psychic, super effective against Psychic and Ghost, weak to Fighting and Bug. Ghost hits Psychic super effectively (fixing the Gen 1 bug).

### Q: How does the Bug Catching Contest work?
**A:** Held at National Park on Tuesdays, Thursdays, and Saturdays. You get 20 Park Balls and can carry one Pokemon. Catch Bug-type Pokemon within a time limit (20 minutes). Judged on rarity, level, HP, and DVs of the caught Pokemon. Prizes: 1st = Sun Stone, 2nd = Everstone, 3rd = Gold Berry. Only one Pokemon can be kept.

### Q: How do Apricorns and Kurt's Balls work?
**A:** Pick Apricorns from trees (one per day per tree, 7 types). Give them to Kurt in Azalea Town. He makes special Poke Balls (one per day): Red = Level Ball, Blue = Lure Ball, Yellow = Moon Ball, Green = Friend Ball, Pink = Love Ball, White = Fast Ball, Black = Heavy Ball. Each has unique catch mechanics.

### Q: How does the Move Deleter work?
**A:** Located in Blackthorn City. He can delete any move, including HMs. This is the only way to remove HM moves from a Pokemon.

### Q: Is there a Move Reminder in Gen 2?
**A:** No. There is no Move Reminder/Relearner in Pokemon Crystal. Once a move is deleted or overwritten, it is gone permanently. Plan your moveset carefully.

### Q: How does the Game Corner work?
**A:** Goldenrod Game Corner has slot machines. Buy coins at 50 for 1000 money. Prizes include TMs and Pokemon (Abra 100 coins, Pikachu 2222 coins, Dratini 2100 coins). Celadon Game Corner (Kanto) also has slot machines with different prizes.

### Q: How does Pokerus work?
**A:** Pokerus is a rare beneficial "virus" that doubles stat experience gained. A Pokemon can catch it randomly after battle (1/21845 chance per Pokemon). It spreads to adjacent party Pokemon over time. After 1-4 days, the Pokemon is cured and can no longer spread it, but keeps the doubled stat exp benefit permanently. Store an infected Pokemon in the PC to preserve the contagious status.

### Q: How does the PC Box system work?
**A:** 14 PC Boxes, each holding 20 Pokemon. You must manually switch boxes when one is full. If the current box is full and you catch a Pokemon, you CANNOT catch it until you switch to a box with space. Always check your box before catching important Pokemon.

### Q: What is the Odd Egg?
**A:** In Crystal, a man at the Goldenrod Day Care gives you the Odd Egg. It can hatch into one of 7 baby Pokemon: Pichu, Cleffa, Igglybuff, Tyrogue, Smoochum, Elekid, or Magby. In the Japanese version, the Odd Egg has a 50% chance of being shiny. In English versions, the shiny rate is approximately 14%.

## Story and Progression

### Q: What order do I fight the gym leaders?
**A:** Johto: (1) Falkner - Violet City - Flying, (2) Bugsy - Azalea Town - Bug, (3) Whitney - Goldenrod City - Normal, (4) Morty - Ecruteak City - Ghost, (5) Chuck - Cianwood City - Fighting, (6) Jasmine - Olivine City - Steel, (7) Pryce - Mahogany Town - Ice, (8) Clair - Blackthorn City - Dragon. Note: Jasmine/Pryce/Chuck have flexible order, but you need Surf for Cianwood (Chuck) and must do the Lighthouse quest for Jasmine.

### Q: How do I get past the Sudowoodo on Route 36?
**A:** Use the Squirtbottle (obtained from the Goldenrod Flower Shop after beating Whitney). It reveals Sudowoodo as a Level 20 Pokemon you can battle and catch.

### Q: What do I do after beating the Elite Four?
**A:** Your quest continues to Kanto. S.S. Aqua takes you to Vermilion City. Collect 8 Kanto badges, then travel to Mt. Silver (Route 28) to face Trainer Red at the summit.

### Q: Who is the final boss? What is Red's team?
**A:** Trainer Red waits at the summit of Mt. Silver. His team: Pikachu (Lv81), Espeon (Lv73), Snorlax (Lv75), Venusaur (Lv77), Charizard (Lv77), Blastoise (Lv77). He is the hardest trainer in the game. Defeating him rolls credits and marks the "true" completion.

### Q: How do I access Mt. Silver?
**A:** You need all 16 badges (8 Johto + 8 Kanto). Then Prof. Oak grants access. Enter through Route 28 (west of the Pokemon League). The entrance guard checks your badges.

### Q: How do I get to Kanto?
**A:** After defeating the Elite Four and entering the Hall of Fame, go to Olivine City Port. Board the S.S. Aqua to Vermilion City. The S.S. Aqua runs only on certain days after your first trip.

### Q: How do I get past the Lake of Rage event?
**A:** Surf to the Red Gyarados in Lake of Rage (forced battle/catch at Level 30, always shiny). After dealing with it, talk to Lance on the north shore. He leads you to the Team Rocket HQ in Mahogany Town (disguised as a souvenir shop). Clear the base, then challenge Pryce at Mahogany Gym.

### Q: How do I solve the Radio Tower Team Rocket event?
**A:** After beating Pryce (7th gym), Team Rocket takes over the Goldenrod Radio Tower. Infiltrate the tower (you need the Basement Key from the Underground). Defeat the Rocket Executives on each floor. On the 5th floor, defeat the final Executive to free the Director. He gives you the Rainbow Wing.

### Q: What are the Wise Trio? What do they give?
**A:** Three monks in Ecruteak City who guard the path to Tin Tower. After obtaining the Rainbow Wing and clearing the Radio Tower event, challenge the Wise Trio. Defeating them grants the Clear Bell, which opens access to Suicune in Tin Tower (Crystal-specific path).

## Common Misconceptions

### Q: Can I catch Mew in Pokemon Crystal?
**A:** No. Mew is not available in Crystal through any legitimate means. It can only be obtained via glitches in Gen 1 and traded over via Time Capsule.

### Q: Can I catch Mewtwo in Pokemon Crystal?
**A:** No. Cerulean Cave (where Mewtwo was in Gen 1) is sealed in Crystal. The cave entrance exists but is permanently blocked.

### Q: Is the Missingno glitch in Pokemon Crystal?
**A:** No. The Missingno glitch is exclusive to Gen 1 (Red/Blue/Yellow). Gen 2 has entirely different code and does not have this exploit.

### Q: Can I get the GS Ball in the English version?
**A:** Originally no -- the GS Ball was Japan-only via Mobile System GB. However, the 3DS Virtual Console re-release (2018) added the GS Ball event worldwide. After entering the Hall of Fame, the GS Ball can be obtained from the Goldenrod Pokemon Center nurse.

### Q: Is the physical/special split the same as Gen 4+?
**A:** No. Gen 2 has NO per-move physical/special assignment. The category is determined entirely by the move's type. All Fire moves are Special, all Fighting moves are Physical, etc. The individual move split was introduced in Gen 4.

### Q: Do abilities exist in Gen 2?
**A:** No. Abilities were introduced in Gen 3 (Ruby/Sapphire). No Pokemon in Crystal has an ability.

### Q: Do natures exist in Gen 2?
**A:** No. Natures were introduced in Gen 3. All stat growth in Gen 2 is based on DVs, stat experience, and base stats only.

### Q: Can you breed Legendary Pokemon?
**A:** No. Legendary Pokemon (including Lugia, Ho-Oh, Suicune, etc.) are in the "No Eggs" Egg Group and cannot breed, even with Ditto.

### Q: Does the Lucky Egg exist in Pokemon Crystal?
**A:** The Lucky Egg item does not function properly in Crystal. Wild Chansey in Crystal do not hold items. This is a common misconception from Gold/Silver where Chansey can hold Lucky Egg.

### Q: Can Pokemon follow you like in Yellow?
**A:** No. The walking Pokemon feature from Yellow is not in Crystal. It returns in HeartGold/SoulSilver.

## Kanto-Specific Questions

### Q: What differences are there between Kanto in Crystal vs. Red/Blue?
**A:** Major changes: Cinnabar Island was destroyed by a volcanic eruption (just a Pokemon Center remains), Seafoam Islands gym replaced Cinnabar Gym (Blaine), Cerulean Cave is sealed, Safari Zone is closed, Viridian Forest is shortened, Mt. Moon is smaller, Power Plant has a quest (find the missing part for the director), Silph Co. is just one floor, many buildings are closed or repurposed.

### Q: What level are the Kanto gym leaders?
**A:** Brock (Rock): 41-44. Misty (Water): 42-47. Lt. Surge (Electric): 44-46. Erika (Grass): 42-46. Janine (Poison, replaces Koga): 36-39. Sabrina (Psychic): 46-48. Blaine (Fire): 45-50. Blue (Mixed): 56-58.

### Q: Where is Blue's gym?
**A:** Viridian City Gym. Blue is the final Kanto gym leader. You must have the other 7 Kanto badges to challenge him.

### Q: Where is the Power Plant and what quest is there?
**A:** Route 10, north of Lavender Town. The Machine Part was stolen by a Team Rocket Grunt hiding in Cerulean Gym. Find and return it to the Power Plant manager to restore power to the Magnet Train and Kanto Radio Tower.

### Q: How do I get the Magnet Train pass?
**A:** After restoring power at the Power Plant, return Copycat's Lost Item (Poke Doll) in Saffron City. She gives you the Pass. The Poke Doll is obtained from a man in the Vermilion Pokemon Fan Club. The Magnet Train connects Goldenrod and Saffron instantly.

### Q: Where do I find the EXPN Card?
**A:** Lavender Radio Tower (Kanto). After restoring power to the Power Plant, talk to the director inside the Lavender Radio Tower. He gives you the EXPN Card, which upgrades your radio to receive Kanto stations and enables the Poke Flute channel to wake Snorlax.

### Q: Is there a sleeping Snorlax in Kanto?
**A:** No. The Diglett's Cave Snorlax from Red/Blue is not in Crystal. There is no wild Snorlax encounter in GSC.

## Battle Strategy

### Q: What is the best team for the Elite Four?
**A:** A balanced team covering all weaknesses: Typhlosion (Fire), Ampharos (Electric), Espeon (Psychic), Heracross (Fighting/Bug), Lapras or Gyarados (Water/Ice), Nidoking or Machamp (coverage). Key moves needed: Ice coverage for Lance's Dragonites, Ground or Water for Rock types, Dark coverage for Will's Psychics.

### Q: What is the best starter?
**A:** Totodile/Feraligatr is generally considered the best for in-game because it gets good physical STAB, learns Ice Punch via TM, and has few disadvantaged gym matchups. Cyndaquil/Typhlosion is also strong but faces a rougher early game. Chikorita/Meganium is the hardest starter due to early gym type disadvantages.

### Q: How do I beat Whitney's Miltank?
**A:** Whitney's Level 20 Miltank knows Rollout, Stomp, Milk Drink, and Attract. Strategies: (1) Use a female Pokemon to avoid Attract. (2) Trade for the Machop in Goldenrod Dept Store -- Fighting resists Rollout. (3) Geodude from Dark Cave (resists Rollout, immune to Stomp). (4) Gastly from Sprout Tower (immune to Stomp and Rollout). (5) Use Sand-Attack or Mud-Slap to lower accuracy.

### Q: How do I beat Clair?
**A:** Clair uses three Dragonair (Level 37-38) and Kingdra (Level 40). Ice moves are key for Dragonair (4x effective). Kingdra is very tanky -- its only weakness is Dragon. Use strong neutral hits, status moves (Thunder Wave), or set up stat boosts. After winning, she refuses to give the badge until you pass the Dragon's Den Shrine quiz.

### Q: How do I beat Red?
**A:** Red's team: Pikachu Lv81, Espeon Lv73, Snorlax Lv75, Venusaur Lv77, Charizard Lv77, Blastoise Lv77. Strategy: Grind to Lv65-75+. Use Earthquake user for Pikachu. Dark/Bug for Espeon. Fighting for Snorlax. Ice for Venusaur. Electric/Rock for Charizard and Blastoise. Bring Max Revives and Full Restores. Snorlax is the hardest member (knows Rest + Snore).

### Q: What is the best grinding spot?
**A:** Before E4: Victory Road trainers, Route 44/45 wild Pokemon (Lv25-30). Post-game: Mt. Silver interior has wild Pokemon up to Lv45-50. The Elite Four itself gives the most exp per run. Rematch trainers via phone also give good exp.

### Q: What are the best TMs to get?
**A:** Key TMs: Return (Normal, high power with friendship), Earthquake (Victory Road), Ice Punch (Goldenrod Dept Store), ThunderPunch (Goldenrod Dept Store), Fire Punch (Goldenrod Dept Store), Shadow Ball (Ecruteak Gym reward), Iron Tail (Jasmine gym reward). Move Tutor in Goldenrod teaches Flamethrower, Ice Beam, and Thunderbolt for 4000 coins each (Wed/Sat only, Crystal exclusive).

## Miscellaneous

### Q: How do I set/reset the clock?
**A:** The clock is set when you start a new game. To reset it, hold Down + Select + B at the title screen. On 3DS Virtual Console, the clock uses the 3DS system clock.

### Q: What are the daily/weekly events?
**A:** Daily: Haircut brothers (Goldenrod Underground), berry trees regrow, Buena's Password changes, Kurt finishes Apricorn balls, Mom may buy items. Weekly: Lucky Number Show drawing (Friday), Bug Catching Contest (Tue/Thu/Sat), Lapras in Union Cave B2F (Friday), Day-of-Week Siblings (each day has a sibling on their specific route who gives an item).

### Q: Who are the Day-of-Week Siblings?
**A:** Seven siblings found across Johto, each on their specific day: Monica (Monday, Route 40, Sharp Beak), Tuscany (Tuesday, Route 29, TwistedSpoon), Wesley (Wednesday, Lake of Rage, Blackbelt), Arthur (Thursday, Route 36, Hard Stone), Frieda (Friday, Route 32, Poison Barb), Santos (Saturday, Blackthorn City, Spell Tag), Sunny (Sunday, Route 37, Magnet).

### Q: How does the Move Tutor work?
**A:** In Crystal, the Move Tutor appears in front of the Goldenrod Game Corner on Wednesdays and Saturdays. He teaches one of three moves: Flamethrower, Ice Beam, or Thunderbolt. Each costs 4000 coins. These are some of the best special moves in the game. Crystal exclusive feature.

### Q: Can I rebattle gym leaders?
**A:** No. Gym leaders can only be fought once each. You cannot rematch them in Crystal. (Rematches were added in HeartGold/SoulSilver.)

### Q: Is there a Battle Tower?
**A:** Yes (Crystal only, not in Gold/Silver). Located west of Olivine/Cianwood via Route 40. You battle 7 trainers in a row with level-restricted teams. No items allowed during battles. In the original Crystal, it required the Mobile Adapter; in the Virtual Console release, it is accessible without it.

### Q: How do I use Mystery Gift?
**A:** On the title screen, choose Mystery Gift (unlocked by talking to the girl in Goldenrod Dept Store 5F). Two Game Boys with infrared sensors point at each other. Each player receives a random decoration or item. The 3DS VC version does not support Mystery Gift between players.

### Q: What are the decorations for my room?
**A:** Mom buys decorations with your savings: NES Console, Super NES, N64, Big Snorlax Doll, Big Lapras Doll, plus various smaller dolls. Some decorations come from Mystery Gift. They are purely cosmetic.

### Q: What happens if I release my starter?
**A:** Nothing special. The game does not prevent you from releasing any Pokemon, including your starter. You will not get a replacement.

### Q: How does the phone system work?
**A:** Register trainer phone numbers after defeating them. They call with tips, item gifts, swarm reports, and rematch requests. Up to 10 contacts. Mom, Prof. Elm, Bill, and Prof. Oak are permanent contacts. See data/mechanics/pokegear.md for full details.

### Q: What is the Radio Card and how do I get it?
**A:** The Radio Card is obtained by answering 5 quiz questions correctly at the Goldenrod Radio Tower front desk. It enables the radio function on your Pokegear with access to Johto stations. Later, the EXPN Card (from Lavender Radio Tower, Kanto) adds Kanto stations.

### Q: How many Pokemon are in Crystal's Pokedex?
**A:** 251 Pokemon total. The Johto Pokedex contains 256 entries (numbered differently from the National Dex). Pokemon #001 (Chikorita) through #251 (Celebi). Not all are obtainable in a single game -- some require trading with Gold/Silver or Gen 1 games.

### Q: Can I trade with Red/Blue/Yellow?
**A:** Yes, via the Time Capsule on the 2nd floor of any Pokemon Center. Restrictions: Only Gen 1 Pokemon can be traded (no Gen 2 Pokemon or moves). The Pokemon must not hold items or know Gen 2-exclusive moves. This is useful for obtaining Mew, version exclusives, and other Gen 1 Pokemon.

### Q: What are the version differences between Crystal and Gold/Silver?
**A:** Crystal additions: Animated Pokemon sprites, Suicune storyline with Eusine, Battle Tower, Move Tutor, Odd Egg, ability to play as a female character, Buena's Password radio show, some NPC changes, Celebi event (GS Ball). Crystal exclusions: Some Pokemon are not available (version exclusives differ from Gold/Silver).
