# Pokemon Crystal - Pokemon Trivia & Obscure Facts

Fun facts, design origins, unique quirks, hidden mechanics, and cut content for Gen 2.

## Design Origins & Name Etymology

### Starters
- **Cyndaquil** — "Cinder" + "quill" (fire hedgehog). Based on the echidna.
- **Totodile** — "Tot" (small) + "crocodile". Baby crocodile design.
- **Chikorita** — "Chicory" (plant) + "-ita" (small, Spanish). Sauropod dinosaur with a leaf.

### Legendary Beasts
- **Raikou** — "Rai" (thunder, Japanese) + "kou" (emperor). Saber-toothed tiger with thunderclouds.
- **Entei** — "En" (flame) + "tei" (emperor). Lion with a volcanic mane.
- **Suicune** — "Sui" (water) + "kune" (from "kunitama," spirit of the land). Aurora borealis mane. Crystal's mascot.

### Legendary Birds
- **Ho-Oh** — "Houou" (phoenix in Japanese). Based on the Fenghuang, a Chinese/Japanese mythological bird.
- **Lugia** — Possibly from Latin "lugeo" (to mourn) or a play on "silver" (its game). Created by Takeshi Shudo for the anime movie first.

### New Type Pokemon
- **Umbreon** — "Umbra" (shadow, Latin) + "eon" (Eevee suffix). Originally intended to be Poison-type, changed to Dark during development.
- **Espeon** — "ESP" (extrasensory perception) + "eon." Cat-like psychic design.
- **Scizor** — "Scissors" + possibly "razor." Red coloring from Metal Coat evolution.
- **Steelix** — "Steel" + "onyx." The original Steel/Ground type.

### Notable Others
- **Wobbuffet** — "That's the way it is!" (Japanese: Sonnansu). Based on a punching bag toy.
- **Smeargle** — "Smear" + "beagle." Painter dog. Sketch copies any move permanently.
- **Unown** — "Unknown." 26 forms representing the Latin alphabet.
- **Shuckle** — "Shuck" (shell) + "turtle." Highest Defense and SpDef base stats possible (230 each) when combined with Power Trick (future gen).
- **Dunsparce** — "Dun" + "dunce" + tsuchinoko (Japanese snake cryptid). One of the most underwhelming Pokemon by design.
- **Delibird** — "Delivery" + "bird." Only move: Present (random damage or healing).
- **Tyrogue** — "Tyro" (beginner) + "rogue." Baby form of the Hitmon family.

## Unique Quirks and Mechanics

### Ditto + Transform
- Ditto transforms into the opposing Pokemon, copying its stats, moves, and type
- In Gen 2, Transform copies the target's DVs (IVs), which affects Shiny status
- Ditto breeding: Ditto can breed with ANY Pokemon that can breed (except other Ditto)
- Ditto is the universal breeding partner — essential for breeding rare males

### Smeargle + Sketch
- Sketch permanently copies the last move used by the opponent
- Can learn ANY move in the game (except Struggle)
- Sketch has 1 PP — use it, then level up to get a new Sketch
- Smeargle can learn Spore (100% accuracy sleep), which is otherwise Paras/Parasect exclusive
- Competitive Smeargle runs Spore + Spikes + Baton Pass (terrifying support set)

### Wobbuffet's Unique Moveset
- Learns only 4 moves ever: Counter, Mirror Coat, Safeguard, Destiny Bond
- Cannot attack directly — only retaliates
- Shadow Tag (ability) traps opponents... but abilities don't exist in Gen 2
- In Gen 2, Wobbuffet is actually not very good — it can't trap opponents

### Unown's Hidden Power
- Unown only knows Hidden Power (can never learn anything else)
- Hidden Power type and base power are determined by DVs
- Different Unown letters have different DV combinations, so different Hidden Power types
- Unown is extremely weak competitively — 336 BST with only one move

### Magikarp's Splash
- Splash does literally nothing (no effect, ever)
- Magikarp can also learn Tackle (Lv15) and Flail (Lv25) in Gen 2
- Despite the memes, Magikarp is not the weakest Pokemon — Sunkern has lower BST (180 vs 200)

### Sunkern — The Weakest Pokemon
- 180 BST — lowest of all 251 Pokemon
- 30 across all stats
- Evolves into Sunflora with Sun Stone (which is... still not very good)

## Shiny Pokemon

### Mechanics
- Shiny status determined by DVs (IVs)
- Shiny DVs: Attack DV must be 2, 3, 6, 7, 10, 11, 14, or 15. Def, Speed, and Special DVs must all be exactly 10.
- Probability: 1/8192 (0.012%)
- Shiny Pokemon have sparkle animation when entering battle
- In Gen 2, shininess is visible in the overworld (different palette)

### Guaranteed Shinies
- **Red Gyarados** at Lake of Rage (always Shiny)
- **Odd Egg** from Pokemon Communication Center has 14% Shiny chance (Crystal only)

### Shiny Breeding
- If one parent is Shiny, offspring have a 1/64 chance of being Shiny
- The Red Gyarados is useful as a Shiny breeding parent
- DVs are partially inherited, increasing Shiny odds with Shiny parents

### Shiny-Locked
No Pokemon are "Shiny-locked" in Gen 2. Every encounter (including gifts and legendaries) can be Shiny.

## Hidden/Cut Content

### Beta Pokemon
- **Ruins of Alph** had an unused puzzle room layout (BetaRuinsOfAlphUnsolvedPuzzleRoom.blk)
- Cut trainer classes and dialogue found in data files
- Several unused map warps exist in the ROM

### Debug Features
- Debug menu exists in the code but is inaccessible in retail
- Can be re-enabled via hacking — provides Pokemon/item editors, warp controls
- The debug build had a "walk through walls" flag

### Spaceworld 1997 Demo
- Early beta (leaked 2018) had drastically different Pokemon designs
- Many Gen 2 Pokemon had completely different sprites and typing
- Baby Pokemon like "Kurstraw" (baby Jynx), "Pudi" (baby Growlithe) were cut
- An entire evolution line for Ditto was planned and cut
- Farfetch'd was supposed to have an evolution

### Unused Items
- GS Ball had no event trigger in non-Japanese Crystal until Virtual Console
- Several key items have placeholder descriptions in the code
- The Teru-sama items are leftover debugging placeholder items

## Interesting Battle Mechanics Trivia

### Sleep Mechanics
- Sleep counter is 1-7 turns (randomly chosen when put to sleep)
- In Gen 2, if you switch out a sleeping Pokemon and switch back in, the sleep counter resets — this is a bug
- Rest always puts you to sleep for exactly 2 turns (you wake up on turn 3)

### Critical Hit Quirks
- Gen 2 crit rate is based on Speed stat: base rate = Speed/512
- Focus Energy DOUBLES the crit rate (it was bugged to divide in Gen 1)
- Scope Lens adds +1 stage to crit rate
- Moves like Slash, Crunch, Cross Chop have +1 crit stage

### Confusion Damage
- Confusion self-hit uses a typeless 40-power physical attack
- It uses your own Attack and Defense for damage calculation
- This means high-Attack Pokemon hurt themselves MORE when confused
- DynamicPunch always confuses but only has 50% accuracy

### Type Effectiveness Oddity
- Ghost is super effective against Psychic in Gen 2 (unlike Gen 1 bug where it was ineffective)
- But the only Ghost-type attacking move with decent power is Shadow Ball (80 power, Special)
- Lick (30 power) and Night Shade (fixed damage) are the only other Ghost attacks

### The Dragon Fang Bug
- Dragon Fang is supposed to boost Dragon-type moves by 10%
- Due to a bug, it actually doesn't boost Dragon-type moves at all
- It boosts... nothing. The item is completely useless in Gen 2.

## Easter Eggs

### Red at Mt. Silver
- The final boss is Red (the protagonist of Gen 1)
- His team: Pikachu Lv81, Espeon Lv73, Snorlax Lv75, Venusaur Lv77, Charizard Lv77, Blastoise Lv77
- Defeating Red rolls credits. The game continues afterward.
- Red does not speak — his only dialogue is "..."

### Professor Oak's Pokemon Talk
- Professor Oak's radio show gives out (mostly useless) Pokemon advice
- Can trigger "Pokemon Music" which plays special remixed music from Gen 1

### Buena's Password (Crystal)
- Daily radio show at 6 PM - midnight
- Listen for the password, then go to the Radio Tower to claim points
- Points exchange for rare items: Rare Candy, Nugget, HP Up, etc.

### Hidden Rocket Admin Imposter
- During the Radio Tower takeover, a Rocket Admin disguises himself as the Radio Tower Director
- If you talk to him in the underground, he reveals himself and battles you

### Trainer House (Viridian City)
- Daily battle against a trainer whose team mirrors the last person you linked/Mystery Gifted with
- If you haven't linked, you fight a default trainer named CAL with:
  Meganium Lv50, Typhlosion Lv50, Feraligatr Lv50

### Mom's Savings
- Your mom saves money from your winnings (if you let her)
- She occasionally buys items for you: Super Potion, Repel, etc.
- She can also buy decorations for your room
- Bug: She sometimes buys useless items at full price
