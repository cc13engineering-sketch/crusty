> Source: Smogon University (smogon.com), GSC OU Viability Rankings, GSC OU Threatlist
> Purpose: Comprehensive competitive Gen 2 analysis for vector database

## Overview

Generation 2 (Gold/Silver/Crystal) competitive play, commonly abbreviated GSC, is defined by a few key characteristics:
- **Snorlax dominance** — the most centralizing Pokemon in any generation's OU tier
- **Spikes-based gameplay** — Spikes is the primary hazard and shapes team building
- **Slow, strategic pace** — fewer sweepers, more walls and pivots
- **No abilities** — abilities didn't exist until Gen 3
- **No held item diversity** — Leftovers on everything (items exist but Leftovers dominates)
- **Physical/Special split by type** — not per move (Ghost is Physical, Dark is Special)

## Key Mechanics for Competitive Play

### Spikes
- Only Cloyster and Forretress learn Spikes
- Up to 3 layers: 1/8, 1/6, 1/4 of max HP on switch-in
- No Stealth Rock in Gen 2 (introduced Gen 4)
- Rapid Spin removes Spikes (Starmie, Forretress, Cloyster are key spinners)
- Spikes + phazing (Roar/Whirlwind) is the primary strategy for wearing teams down

### Sleep Mechanics
- Sleep Talk: Use a random move while asleep (key for RestTalk strategy)
- Only 1 Pokemon can be put to sleep per team (Sleep Clause)
- Sleep lasts 1-7 turns (random)
- Rest: Fully heals HP + status, puts user to sleep for 2 turns exactly

### Critical Hits
- Base rate: ~6.25% (1/16)
- Focus Energy + Scope Lens can increase rate significantly
- Critical hits in Gen 2 ignore negative stat changes on attacker AND positive stat changes on defender

### Leftovers
- Recovers 1/16 of max HP per turn
- Almost every competitive Pokemon holds Leftovers
- No item clause in standard play (multiple Leftovers allowed)

### Baton Pass
- Passes stat boosts and substitutes to the next Pokemon
- Key users: Espeon (Growth), Jolteon (Agility), Vaporeon (Acid Armor)
- Baton Pass chains are viable but fragile

## Key Threats Analysis

### Snorlax — "The King"
**Why it's broken:**
- 160 HP / 110 SpDef makes it the best special wall
- 110 Attack lets it hit hard physically
- Curse boosts Attack + Defense at the cost of Speed (already slow — doesn't matter)
- Belly Drum can maximize Attack in one turn
- Movepool: Double-Edge, Earthquake, Fire Blast, Self-Destruct, Body Slam, Rest, Sleep Talk
- Every team must have a Snorlax answer or lose

**Common Sets:**
1. **CurseLax**: Curse / Double-Edge / Earthquake / Rest or Fire Blast
2. **DrumLax**: Belly Drum / Double-Edge / Earthquake / Rest
3. **MixLax**: Double-Edge / Earthquake / Fire Blast / Self-Destruct or Rest
4. **RestTalkLax**: Rest / Sleep Talk / Double-Edge / Earthquake or Curse

**Counters:** Machamp (Cross Chop), Heracross (Megahorn), Skarmory (walls physical), Tyranitar (Pursuit on switch)

### Skarmory — "The Wall"
**Why it's elite:**
- 140 Defense walls almost all physical attackers
- Whirlwind forces switches (racks up Spikes damage)
- Steel/Flying typing gives many resistances + immunities
- Only weak to Fire and Electric

**Counters:** Raikou (Thunderbolt), Zapdos (Thunder), Gengar (can't be Whirlwinded as Ghost)

### Raikou — "The Pivot"
**Why it's elite:**
- 115 SpA + 115 Speed = fast and strong
- Roar forces switches (Spikes damage)
- RestTalk makes it hard to wear down
- Checks more than half the metagame

**Counters:** Snorlax (tanks Thunderbolt), Tyranitar (resists Electric), Steelix (Ground immunity to Electric... wait no, Steel/Ground takes neutral from Electric)

## Banned Moves and Clauses

### Standard Clauses (Smogon)
- **Sleep Clause**: Only 1 opponent Pokemon may be put to sleep at a time
- **Species Clause**: No duplicate Pokemon on a team
- **Evasion Clause**: Double Team and Minimize are banned
- **OHKO Clause**: Fissure, Horn Drill, Guillotine are banned
- **Freeze Clause**: Only 1 Pokemon may be frozen at a time (sometimes applied)

### Notable Banned/Restricted
- No Pokemon from the Ubers list (Mewtwo, Mew, Lugia, Ho-Oh, Celebi)
- Swagger + Paralysis combo ("SwagPlay") is controversial but not officially banned in GSC
- Baton Pass is fully legal in standard GSC OU

## Meta-Game Evolution

### Early GSC Meta (late 1990s - early 2000s)
- Dominated by Snorlax + Zapdos
- Spikes was underrated initially
- Stall teams were less refined

### Modern GSC Meta (2010s - present)
- Spikes is recognized as the most important move
- Forretress + Cloyster valued highly for Spikes support
- Raikou rose to become the #2 Pokemon (best shuffler)
- Perish trap strategies became more refined
- Team building is highly optimized around Snorlax matchup
- ~20-25 truly viable OU Pokemon (small metagame by modern standards)

### Design Philosophy
GSC rewards:
- **Patience**: Games are long; chip damage from Spikes/Toxic adds up
- **Prediction**: Switching is critical; wrong switches eat Spikes + attacks
- **Team synergy**: Every member must handle specific threats
- **Risk management**: Explosion, Self-Destruct, and Belly Drum are high-risk/high-reward
