## Pre-E4 Preparation

### Recommended Level Range: 42-50
- Will's strongest is Lv42, Lance's Dragonite is Lv50
- Having your team at Lv45-48 provides comfortable margins
- Under-leveled runs (Lv38-42) are possible but require good strategy and items

### Essential Items to Buy
| Item | Quantity | Cost | Purpose |
|------|----------|------|---------|
| Full Restore | 20-30 | $3000 each | Full HP + status heal |
| Revive | 10-15 | $1500 each | Emergency recovery |
| Max Potion | 10 | $2500 each | HP recovery backup |
| Full Heal | 10 | $600 each | Status only (cheaper) |
| Ice Heal | 5 | $250 each | Karen's Houndoom trap |

**Total budget:** ~$100,000-150,000

### Essential Moves
- **Ice Beam / Blizzard** — For Lance's 3 Dragonite
- **Thunderbolt** — For Lance's Gyarados + general coverage
- **Earthquake** — Universal physical coverage
- **Psychic** — For Koga's Poison types
- **Surf** — Required HM + good coverage

### TM Sources
- Ice Beam: Goldenrod Game Corner (costs coins) or Mahogany Gym
- Thunderbolt: Route 10 Power Plant area
- Earthquake: Victory Road (TM26)
- Psychic: Saffron City or Game Corner
- Surf: HM03 from Ecruteak

## Elite Four #1: Will (Psychic)

### Will's Team
| Pokemon | Level | Type | Moves | Strategy Notes |
|---------|-------|------|-------|----------------|
| Xatu | 40 | Psychic/Flying | Confuse Ray, Future Sight, Quick Attack, Psychic | Confuse Ray is annoying |
| Jynx | 41 | Ice/Psychic | DoubleSlap, Ice Punch, Lovely Kiss, Psychic | Lovely Kiss = sleep |
| Exeggutor | 41 | Grass/Psychic | Reflect, Leech Seed, Psychic, Egg Bomb | Leech Seed + Reflect stalls |

| Xatu | 42 | Psychic/Flying | Confuse Ray, Future Sight, Quick Attack, Psychic | Slightly higher level |

### Weaknesses by Pokemon
- Xatu: Electric, Ice, Rock, Ghost, Dark (5 weaknesses)
- Jynx: Fire, Rock, Bug, Ghost, Dark, Steel (6 weaknesses)
- Exeggutor: Fire, Ice (4x), Flying, Poison, Bug (4x), Ghost, Dark
- Slowbro: Electric, Grass, Bug, Ghost, Dark

### Strategy
- **Dark-type moves** hit everything super-effectively. Umbreon with Pursuit/Faint Attack works.
- **Bug-type moves** (Megahorn from Heracross) are devastating — Exeggutor takes 4x.
- Jynx: Hit fast before Lovely Kiss (sleep). Fire or Rock attacks OHKO.
- Slowbro: DO NOT let it use Curse + Amnesia. Hit hard with Electric/Grass immediately.
- Exeggutor: Fire or Ice Beam. Any Ice move does 4x damage.

### Recommended Lead: Umbreon (Dark), Heracross (Bug/Fighting), or Typhlosion (Fire)

**Difficulty: 3/10**

## Elite Four #2: Koga (Poison)

### Koga's Team
| Pokemon | Level | Type | Moves | Strategy Notes |
|---------|-------|------|-------|----------------|
| Ariados | 40 | Bug/Poison | Double Team, Giga Drain, Baton Pass, Spider Web | Traps + passes evasion |
| Venomoth | 41 | Bug/Poison | Gust, Supersonic, Psychic, Toxic | Toxic + Supersonic combo |
| Forretress | 43 | Bug/Steel | Protect, Swift, Explosion, Spikes | Spikes chip + Explosion |
| Muk | 42 | Poison | Minimize, Toxic, Sludge Bomb, Acid Armor | Evasion stacking nightmare |
| Crobat | 44 | Poison/Flying | Double Team, Wing Attack, Toxic, Confuse Ray | Fast, evasive, annoying |

### Weaknesses by Pokemon
- Ariados: Fire, Flying, Rock, Psychic
- Venomoth: Fire, Flying, Rock, Psychic
- Forretress: Fire (4x only weakness!)
- Muk: Psychic, Ground
- Crobat: Electric, Ice, Psychic, Rock

### Strategy
- **Koga is annoying, not hard.** His team stalls with Toxic, Double Team, and confusion.
- Bring **Full Heals / Full Restores** — status is Koga's main weapon.
- **Forretress**: Only weak to Fire (4x). Flamethrower/Fire Blast one-shots. Beware Explosion (huge damage, KOs Forretress too).
- **Muk**: Minimize makes it nearly unhittable. Use Swift (never misses), or high-accuracy moves. Earthquake/Psychic before it sets up.
- **Ariados** can trap with Spider Web and Baton Pass Double Team boosts. KO it fast.
- **Psychic-type moves** hit 4 of 5 Pokemon super-effectively. Alakazam or Espeon sweeps.
- **Ground moves** (Earthquake) hit Muk, and Fire handles the rest.

### Recommended Lead: Alakazam/Espeon (Psychic) or Typhlosion (Fire)

**Difficulty: 4/10** (annoying evasion/status, but not powerful)

## Elite Four #3: Bruno (Fighting)

### Bruno's Team
| Pokemon | Level | Type | Moves | Strategy Notes |
|---------|-------|------|-------|----------------|
| Hitmontop | 42 | Fighting | Quick Attack, Pursuit, Counter, Detect | Counter reflects double damage |
| Hitmonlee | 42 | Fighting | Hi Jump Kick, Foresight, Swagger, Double Kick | Hi Jump Kick crashes on miss |
| Hitmonchan | 42 | Fighting | ThunderPunch, Ice Punch, Fire Punch, Mach Punch | Elemental punches = surprise coverage |
| Onix | 43 | Rock/Ground | Bind, Rock Slide, Earthquake, Sandstorm | Not Fighting; different weaknesses |
| Machamp | 46 | Fighting | Rock Slide, Foresight, Vital Throw, Cross Chop | Highest level, heavy hitter |

### Weaknesses by Pokemon
- Hitmontop/Hitmonlee/Hitmonchan/Machamp: Flying, Psychic
- Onix: Water (4x), Grass (4x), Ice, Fighting, Ground, Steel

### Strategy
- **Psychic or Flying** moves sweep most of Bruno's team.
- **Hitmontop**: Beware Counter — if you use physical moves, it reflects double damage. Use special attacks.
- **Hitmonlee**: Hi Jump Kick has crash damage on miss. It can hurt itself.
- **Hitmonchan**: The elemental punches (Thunder/Ice/Fire Punch) are all Special in Gen 2. They can surprise type-resistant Pokemon. Flying-types beware of ThunderPunch; Ice-types beware of Fire Punch.
- **Onix**: Surf one-shots (4x effective). Not a Fighting type — different counter needed.
- **Machamp** at Lv46: Highest level. Cross Chop is devastating. Foresight removes Ghost immunity. Psychic one-shots.

### Recommended Lead: Alakazam/Espeon (Psychic sweeps 4/5), Pidgeot/Crobat (Flying for Fighting coverage)

**Difficulty: 3/10** (straightforward type matchups)

## Elite Four #4: Karen (Dark)

### Karen's Team
| Pokemon | Level | Type | Moves | Strategy Notes |
|---------|-------|------|-------|----------------|
| Umbreon | 42 | Dark | Confuse Ray, Sand-Attack, Faint Attack, Mean Look | Stall + trap combo |
| Vileplume | 42 | Grass/Poison | Petal Dance, Stun Spore, Acid, Moonlight | Stun Spore → Petal Dance |
| Gengar | 45 | Ghost/Poison | Lick, Spite, Curse, Destiny Bond | Destiny Bond KOs your Pokemon |
| Murkrow | 44 | Dark/Flying | Whirlwind, Pursuit, Faint Attack, Quick Attack | Whirlwind disrupts setup |
| Houndoom | 47 | Dark/Fire | Flamethrower, Crunch, Pursuit, Roar | Highest level; strong attacker |

### Weaknesses by Pokemon
- Umbreon: Fighting, Bug
- Vileplume: Fire, Ice, Flying, Psychic
- Gengar: Ghost, Dark, Psychic, Ground (but Levitate... no, Gen 2 no abilities)
- Murkrow: Electric, Ice, Rock
- Houndoom: Fighting, Ground, Rock, Water

### Strategy
- Karen has the most diverse team. No single type counters everything.
- **Umbreon**: Tanky staller. Confuse Ray + Sand-Attack + Mean Look traps and annoys. Use Fighting moves (Machamp) or just powerful neutral hits. It will take a while to go down.
- **Vileplume**: Stun Spore is dangerous. Fire Blast or Psychic one-shots. Don't let it get Stun Spore off.
- **Gengar**: **BEWARE DESTINY BOND.** If you KO Gengar while Destiny Bond is active, your Pokemon faints too. Use weaker attacks to bait it, or attack the turn after Destiny Bond (it only lasts one turn). Curse also drains HP per turn.
- **Murkrow**: Weakest member. Whirlwind can force switches. Electric/Ice moves handle it.
- **Houndoom** at Lv47: Karen's ace. Flamethrower hits hard; Crunch is STAB; Pursuit punishes switching. Water or Fighting moves are best. Earthquake works if you have it.

### Recommended Lead: Machamp (Fighting for Umbreon/Houndoom), Feraligatr (Water for Houndoom)

**Difficulty: 5/10** (Destiny Bond + Umbreon stalling can be tricky)

## Champion: Lance (Dragon)

### Lance's Team
| Pokemon | Level | Type | Moves | Strategy Notes |
|---------|-------|------|-------|----------------|
| Gyarados | 44 | Water/Flying | Flail, Rain Dance, Surf, Hyper Beam | Sets up Rain Dance |
| Dragonite | 47 | Dragon/Flying | Thunder Wave, Twister, Thunder, Hyper Beam | Uses Thunder in Rain |
| Dragonite | 47 | Dragon/Flying | Thunder Wave, Twister, Thunder, Hyper Beam | Duplicate of above |
| Aerodactyl | 46 | Rock/Flying | Wing Attack, AncientPower, Rock Slide, Hyper Beam | Fast physical attacker |
| Dragonite | 50 | Dragon/Flying | Fire Blast, Safeguard, Outrage, Hyper Beam | Ace Dragonite; highest level |
| Charizard | 46 | Fire/Flying | Flamethrower, Wing Attack, Slash, Hyper Beam | Fire coverage |

### Weaknesses by Pokemon
- Gyarados: Electric (4x), Rock
- Dragonite x3: Ice (4x), Dragon, Rock, Electric
- Aerodactyl: Water, Electric, Ice, Rock, Steel
- Charizard: Water, Electric, Rock (4x)

### Strategy
- **Ice and Electric are the keys to this fight.**
- **Gyarados**: Thunder or Thunderbolt one-shots (4x weakness). It uses Rain Dance which boosts Thunder accuracy to 100% — this helps your Electric moves too!
- **Dragonite x3**: ICE BEAM. All three take 4x damage from Ice. One Ice Beam from a decent SpA Pokemon (Lapras, Jynx, Piloswine) OHKOs each one. They use Thunder Wave for paralysis — bring Parlyz Heals.
- **Aerodactyl**: Fast (Lv46, high Speed). Rock Slide can flinch. Ice Beam or Surf handles it. Electric works too.
- **Dragonite Lv50 (ace)**: Has Fire Blast and Outrage instead of Thunder. Outrage is devastating but causes self-confusion after 2-3 turns. Safeguard blocks status. Ice Beam still one-shots.
- **Charizard**: Surf, Thunderbolt, or Rock Slide. Rock is 4x effective. Stealth Rock would help... but doesn't exist in Gen 2.
- **Hyper Beam** on every Pokemon: After using it, the Pokemon recharges for one turn. This is a free turn to heal or set up.

### The Ice Beam Strategy
If you have ONE Pokemon with Ice Beam and decent Special Attack (Lapras ideal):
1. Use Ice Beam on Dragonite #1 → OHKO
2. Use Ice Beam on Dragonite #2 → OHKO
3. Use Ice Beam on Dragonite #3 → OHKO
4. Thunderbolt for Gyarados/Charizard
5. Surf for Aerodactyl

**Lapras** (Union Cave B2F Friday) with Ice Beam + Thunderbolt + Surf + Psychic can theoretically solo Lance.

### Recommended Team for Lance:
1. Electric type with Thunderbolt (Ampharos, Raikou, Magneton)
2. Ice type with Ice Beam (Lapras, Piloswine, Jynx)
3. Water type for backup (Feraligatr, Gyarados)
4. Any strong backup for emergencies

**Difficulty: 6/10** (3 Dragonite is scary on paper, but Ice Beam trivializes it)
