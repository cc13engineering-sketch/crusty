## Johto Gyms

### Gym 1: Falkner (Flying) — Violet City

**Falkner's Team:**
| Pokemon | Level | Moves | HP (approx) |
|---------|-------|-------|-------------|
| Pidgey | 7 | Tackle, Mud-Slap, Gust | ~24 |
| Pidgeotto | 9 | Tackle, Mud-Slap, Gust | ~34 |

**Gym Trainers:** Bird Keeper Abe (Spearow Lv9), Bird Keeper Rod (Pidgey Lv7 x2)

**Weaknesses:** Electric, Rock, Ice
**Resistances:** Grass, Bug, Fighting (immune to Ground)

**Strategy:**
- Easiest gym. Most starters at Lv10+ can power through.
- Geodude from Dark Cave (Route 31/46) walls everything with Rock-type resistance.
- Trade Bellsprout for Onix in Violet City for an easy win.
- Mud-Slap lowers accuracy — heal status if it stacks.
- If using Chikorita, overleveling to Lv10+ makes it manageable despite the type disadvantage.

**Recommended Counter Pokemon:** Geodude (Lv6+), Onix (trade), Mareep (if backtracked to Route 32)

**Difficulty: 1/10**

### Gym 2: Bugsy (Bug) — Azalea Town

**Bugsy's Team:**
| Pokemon | Level | Moves | HP (approx) |
|---------|-------|-------|-------------|
| Metapod | 14 | Tackle, String Shot, Harden | ~42 |
| Kakuna | 14 | Poison Sting, String Shot, Harden | ~40 |
| Scyther | 16 | Fury Cutter, Quick Attack, Leer | ~54 |

**Gym Trainers:** Bug Catchers Al, Benny, Josh

**Weaknesses:** Fire, Flying, Rock
**Key Threat:** Scyther's Fury Cutter doubles damage each consecutive hit (10→20→40→80→160)

**Strategy:**
- Metapod and Kakuna are harmless. Save your strong Pokemon for Scyther.
- **Critical**: Do NOT let Fury Cutter hit consecutively. Switch Pokemon to reset the counter.
- Cyndaquil/Quilava destroys this gym with Ember.
- Pidgeotto (Flying) is strong but watch out for Fury Cutter stacking.
- Geodude resists everything Scyther has.

**Recommended Counter Pokemon:** Quilava (Lv14+), Geodude/Onix, Pidgeotto, any Fire type

**Difficulty: 3/10** (Scyther can surprise you if unprepared)

### Gym 3: Whitney (Normal) — Goldenrod City

**Whitney's Team:**
| Pokemon | Level | Moves | HP (approx) |
|---------|-------|-------|-------------|
| Clefairy | 18 | Encore, DoubleSlap, Mimic, Metronome | ~63 |
| Miltank | 20 | Stomp, Attract, Milk Drink, Rollout | ~80 |

**Gym Trainers:** Beauty Victoria, Beauty Samantha, Lass Carrie, Lass Bridget

**Weaknesses:** Fighting (Normal has no resistances except Ghost immunity)
**Key Threats:** Miltank's Rollout (doubles each turn, can OHKO after 3-4 hits), Attract (male Pokemon can't attack 50% of time), Milk Drink (heals 50% HP)

**Strategy:**
- **The hardest early gym.** Many players lose here.
- Clefairy's Encore can lock you into a bad move; Metronome is unpredictable.
- **Miltank**: Attract only affects male Pokemon. Use a **female** Pokemon if possible.
- **Rollout strategy**: The move doubles each turn and has 90% accuracy. Interrupt it by making Miltank faint, miss, or switch. If it connects 5 times, it can KO anything.
- Trade Drowzee for **Machop** in Goldenrod Dept Store — Machop is Fighting-type and female, countering both Attract and Normal typing.
- Geodude/Graveler resists Stomp/Rollout and can hit back with Rock Throw.
- Status moves (Paralysis, Sleep) can stop the Rollout chain.
- Milk Drink means you need burst damage, not chip damage.

**Recommended Counter Pokemon:** Machop (trade, female, Fighting), Geodude/Graveler, Heracross (headbutt), any female Pokemon with strong attacks

**Difficulty: 7/10** (hardest gym in early game)

**Note:** Whitney cries after losing. You must talk to the Lass in the gym to receive the badge.

### Gym 4: Morty (Ghost) — Ecruteak City

**Morty's Team:**
| Pokemon | Level | Moves | HP (approx) |
|---------|-------|-------|-------------|
| Gastly | 21 | Lick, Spite, Mean Look, Curse | ~45 |
| Haunter | 21 | Hypnosis, Mimic, Curse, Night Shade | ~55 |
| Haunter | 23 | Hypnosis, Mimic, Curse, Night Shade | ~58 |
| Gengar | 25 | Hypnosis, Shadow Ball, Mean Look, Dream Eater | ~72 |

**Weaknesses:** Ghost, Dark (but Ghost is Physical in Gen 2, Dark is Special)
**Resistances:** Poison, Bug (immune to Normal, Fighting)

**Strategy:**
- Normal and Fighting moves are completely useless (immunity).
- **Hypnosis** is the biggest threat — bring Awakenings or use Chesto Berry/Mint Berry.
- Gengar's combo: Hypnosis → Dream Eater (heals Gengar). Mean Look traps you.
- Gen 2 quirk: Ghost moves (Shadow Ball, Lick) use the Attack stat. Dark moves (Bite, Crunch) use Special Attack.
- **Best counters**: Pokemon with Bite (Raticate, Croconaw, Gyarados) or Crunch, or high-level Pokemon that can tank hits.
- Umbreon (if you evolved Eevee at night) has Dark typing and resists Ghost moves.
- Kadabra with high SpA can use non-Ghost/non-Normal moves effectively.

**Recommended Counter Pokemon:** Umbreon, anything with Bite/Crunch, Kadabra (Psych Up or raw power), Raticate (Hyper Fang + Bite)

**Difficulty: 5/10** (annoying Hypnosis spam, but manageable)

### Gym 5: Chuck (Fighting) — Cianwood City

**Chuck's Team:**
| Pokemon | Level | Moves | HP (approx) |
|---------|-------|-------|-------------|
| Primeape | 27 | Leer, Rage, Karate Chop, Fury Swipes | ~82 |
| Poliwrath | 30 | Hypnosis, Mind Reader, Surf, DynamicPunch | ~105 |

**Weaknesses:** Flying, Psychic (Poliwrath also weak to Electric, Grass)
**Key Threat:** Poliwrath's Hypnosis → Mind Reader → DynamicPunch (never misses after Mind Reader, causes Confusion)

**Strategy:**
- Primeape is straightforward — any Flying or Psychic type handles it.
- **Poliwrath** is the real fight. Water/Fighting with Hypnosis makes it tricky.
- Hypnosis → Mind Reader → guaranteed DynamicPunch. This confuses + deals heavy damage.
- Bring Awakenings. Status heals are critical.
- Electric types (Ampharos, Magneton) hit Poliwrath super-effectively.
- Grass types (Meganium, Victreebel) are 4x effective but watch Surf.
- Kadabra/Alakazam with Psychic one-shots most of Chuck's team.

**Recommended Counter Pokemon:** Kadabra, Ampharos, Pidgeotto/Pidgeot, any Psychic or Electric

**Difficulty: 4/10**

### Gym 6: Jasmine (Steel) — Olivine City

**Jasmine's Team:**
| Pokemon | Level | Moves | HP (approx) |
|---------|-------|-------|-------------|
| Magnemite | 30 | Thunderbolt, Supersonic, SonicBoom, Thunder Wave | ~68 |
| Magnemite | 30 | Thunderbolt, Supersonic, SonicBoom, Thunder Wave | ~68 |
| Steelix | 35 | Iron Tail, Screech, Sunny Day, Rock Throw | ~115 |

**Weaknesses:** Fire, Fighting, Ground (Magnemite: Ground 4x; Steelix: Fire, Water, Fighting, Ground)
**Key Threat:** Steelix has massive Defense (200 base); Screech halves your Defense; Iron Tail hits hard

**Strategy:**
- Magnemites are weak — Earthquake or any Ground move is 4x effective. Even Dig works.
- Steelix: DO NOT use physical attacks unless you have very high Attack. Its Defense is enormous.
- **Special attacks are key**: Surf, Flamethrower, special-side Fire/Water moves.
- Typhlosion with Flamethrower/Fire Blast demolishes this gym.
- Feraligatr with Surf is effective.
- Steelix sets up Sunny Day (weakens Water, boosts Fire?) — this actually helps Fire-type challengers.
- Quagsire (Water/Ground) is immune to Thunderbolt and hits everything SE.

**Recommended Counter Pokemon:** Typhlosion, Feraligatr/Croconaw, Quagsire, anything with Earthquake, Machoke

**Difficulty: 4/10** (predictable, but Steelix is a wall if unprepared)

### Gym 7: Pryce (Ice) — Mahogany Town

**Pryce's Team:**
| Pokemon | Level | Moves | HP (approx) |
|---------|-------|-------|-------------|
| Seel | 27 | Headbutt, Icy Wind, Rest, Snore | ~85 |
| Dewgong | 29 | Aurora Beam, Ice Beam, Rest, Sleep Talk | ~105 |
| Piloswine | 31 | Blizzard, Icy Wind, Fury Attack, Mist | ~110 |

**Weaknesses:** Fire, Fighting, Rock, Steel (Piloswine: Water, Grass, Fighting, Steel — 4x to each except Fire)
**Key Threat:** Dewgong's Rest + Sleep Talk combo makes it annoyingly tanky; Piloswine's Blizzard hits hard

**Strategy:**
- Seel is easy. Just hit it hard before it Rests.
- Dewgong: Rest + Sleep Talk means it heals and attacks while sleeping. Bring strong attackers to overwhelm it.
- Piloswine: Ice/Ground typing. 4x weak to Water and Grass. Surf or Razor Leaf one-shots.
- Typhlosion resists Ice but watch Piloswine's Ground typing (neutral to Fire).
- Machoke/Machamp with Fighting moves cleans up.
- Red Gyarados works well here.

**Recommended Counter Pokemon:** Feraligatr (Surf), Ampharos, Machoke, Red Gyarados, Typhlosion

**Difficulty: 3/10**

### Gym 8: Clair (Dragon) — Blackthorn City

**Clair's Team:**
| Pokemon | Level | Moves | HP (approx) |
|---------|-------|-------|-------------|
| Dragonair | 37 | Thunder Wave, Surf, DragonBreath, Slam | ~120 |
| Dragonair | 37 | Thunder Wave, Thunderbolt, DragonBreath, Slam | ~120 |
| Dragonair | 37 | Thunder Wave, Ice Beam, DragonBreath, Slam | ~120 |
| Kingdra | 40 | Surf, Hyper Beam, DragonBreath, SmokeScreen | ~140 |

**Weaknesses:** Dragon, Ice (Dragonair); Dragon only (Kingdra — Water/Dragon has almost no weaknesses)
**Key Threat:** Kingdra. Water/Dragon typing means only Dragon-type moves are super-effective. SmokeScreen lowers accuracy.

**Strategy:**
- **Hardest gym in Johto.** Clair's team is high-level and well-constructed.
- Each Dragonair has different coverage (Surf, Thunderbolt, Ice Beam) — type counters are unreliable.
- Thunder Wave from all 3 Dragonair means paralysis is constant. Bring Parlyz Heals.
- **Kingdra** is the real boss. Weak ONLY to Dragon. In Gen 2, the only Dragon moves available are DragonBreath (60 power) and Outrage (120 but confusion).
- Strategy: Overpower Kingdra with high-level neutral attacks. STAB Hyper Beam from Kingdra hurts.
- **Lapras with Ice Beam** handles the 3 Dragonair easily; use brute force on Kingdra.
- Piloswine/Jynx from Ice Path provide Ice coverage.
- SmokeScreen + Hyper Beam on Kingdra — use X Accuracy items or moves that can't miss.

**Recommended Counter Pokemon:** Lapras (Ice Beam for Dragonair, tank Kingdra), Piloswine, Jynx, any Lv40+ Pokemon with strong neutral moves

**Difficulty: 8/10**

**Note:** After winning, Clair refuses the badge. Must complete Dragon's Den quiz to receive Rising Badge.
