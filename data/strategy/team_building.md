## Pokemon Availability by Gym

### Before Gym 1 (Falkner, Flying — Violet City)
**Available Pokemon** (catchable before challenging Falkner):
- **Starter**: Chikorita / Cyndaquil / Totodile (Lv5)
- **Route 29**: Pidgey, Sentret, Rattata, Hoppip, Hoothoot (night)
- **Route 30**: Pidgey, Caterpie, Weedle, Ledyba (morn), Spinarak (night), Metapod
- **Route 31**: Pidgey, Bellsprout, Caterpie/Weedle, Poliwag (night)
- **Sprout Tower**: Gastly (night), Rattata
- **Dark Cave**: Geodude, Zubat (needs Flash — available from Sprout Tower)
- **Trade**: Bellsprout for Onix ("Rocky") in Violet City

**Best choices for Falkner**: Geodude (Rock resists Flying), Onix (trade), Mareep (Route 32, but past gym). Cyndaquil or Totodile are neutral matchups. Chikorita is weak here.

### Before Gym 2 (Bugsy, Bug — Azalea Town)
**New additions** (Route 32, Union Cave, Route 33):
- **Route 32**: Ekans, Bellsprout, Mareep, Wooper (night), Hoppip
- **Union Cave**: Geodude, Onix, Sandshrew, Zubat
- **Route 33**: Rattata, Spearow, Hoppip, Zubat (night)

**Best choices for Bugsy**: Geodude (resists Bug), Pidgey/Pidgeotto (Flying beats Bug), Cyndaquil/Quilava (Fire beats Bug). Watch out for Scyther's Fury Cutter doubling each hit.

### Before Gym 3 (Whitney, Normal — Goldenrod City)
**New additions** (Ilex Forest, Route 34):
- **Ilex Forest**: Oddish, Paras, Caterpie, Metapod, Zubat (night)
- **Route 34**: Drowzee, Rattata, Abra, Ditto
- **Goldenrod trades/gifts**: Machop (trade Drowzee at Dept Store)
- **Game Corner**: Abra (200 coins), Dratini (2100 coins)

**Best choices for Whitney**: **Machop** (trade in Goldenrod — Fighting beats Normal; comes with Gold Berry), Geodude/Graveler (resists Rollout, immune to Stomp), Female Pokemon (immune to Attract). Whitney's Miltank is notorious — use physical walls, Fighting types, or status moves.

### Before Gym 4 (Morty, Ghost — Ecruteak City)
**New additions** (Routes 35-37, National Park):
- **Route 35**: Nidoran-F, Nidoran-M, Drowzee, Abra, Yanma (rare 1%), Ditto
- **National Park**: Caterpie, Scyther/Pinsir (Bug Contest), Sunkern
- **Route 36**: Growlithe, Stantler, Bellsprout, Pidgey
- **Route 37**: Growlithe, Stantler, Pidgey/Pidgeotto

**Best choices for Morty**: Normal-type moves are immune to Ghost, but Ghost is immune to Normal too. Use Dark-type moves (Bite, Crunch, Pursuit) or just raw power. Umbreon, Murkrow, or anything with Bite works. Note: in Gen 2, Ghost is Physical and Dark is Special.

### Before Gym 5 (Chuck, Fighting — Cianwood City)
**New additions** (Routes 38-41):
- **Route 38-39**: Raticate, Tauros, Miltank, Magnemite, Farfetch'd, Meowth (night)
- **Olivine area**: Tentacool/Tentacruel (Surf)
- **Route 40-41**: Tentacool, Mantine (rare)

**Best choices for Chuck**: Flying (Pidgeotto/Pidgeot), Psychic (Drowzee/Hypno, Kadabra), Grass types. Poliwrath knows Hypnosis + Mind Reader + DynamicPunch combo — very dangerous.

### Before Gym 6 (Jasmine, Steel — Olivine City)
**Same pool** as Gym 5 plus anything caught getting to/from Cianwood.

**Best choices for Jasmine**: Fire (Quilava/Typhlosion, Growlithe), Fighting (Machop/Machoke), Ground (Geodude/Graveler/Golem, Sandshrew). Steelix has high Defense — special attacks work better.

### Before Gym 7 (Pryce, Ice — Mahogany Town)
**New additions** (Route 42-43):
- **Route 42**: Spearow, Zubat, Mareep, Mankey, Flaaffy
- **Mt. Mortar**: Machop, Machoke, Geodude, Graveler, Tyrogue (Karate King)
- **Route 43**: Pidgeotto, Flaaffy, Girafarig, Venonat (night)
- **Lake of Rage**: Red Gyarados (guaranteed shiny Lv30)

**Best choices for Pryce**: Fire (Typhlosion, Growlithe/Arcanine), Fighting (Machoke/Machamp), Electric (Ampharos, Magneton). Red Gyarados works well too.

### Before Gym 8 (Clair, Dragon — Blackthorn City)
**New additions** (Route 44, Ice Path):
- **Route 44**: Weepinbell, Tangela, Lickitung, Bellsprout, Poliwag (night)
- **Ice Path**: Swinub, Jynx, Delibird

**Best choices for Clair**: Ice moves are king — Ice Beam, Blizzard, Icy Wind. Piloswine (Ice Path), Jynx (Ice Path), Lapras (Union Cave Friday). Kingdra is Water/Dragon — only weak to Dragon. Use Dragon-type moves or brute force with strong neutral hits.

## Type Coverage Analysis

### Best Type Combinations for Johto Playthrough

| Combo | Covers (SE) | Weak Gyms | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Water/Fire/Grass | 7 types SE | Good neutral | Classic starter triangle |
| Water/Electric/Ground | 9 types SE | Almost everything | Surf + Thunderbolt + Earthquake |
| Water/Fighting/Flying | 8 types SE | All gyms viable | Versatile coverage |
| Fire/Ice/Electric | 8 types SE | Covers Dragon | Great E4 prep |

### Key Types to Have on Your Team

1. **Water** (Surf is required HM; covers Fire, Ground, Rock) — Feraligatr, Lapras, Gyarados, Red Gyarados
2. **Electric** (covers Water, Flying) — Ampharos (Mareep from Route 32), Magneton, Raikou (post-Burned Tower)
3. **Fighting or Ground** (covers Normal, Steel, Rock) — Machoke, Primeape, Golem
4. **Ice** (covers Dragon, Flying, Grass, Ground) — Lapras, Piloswine, Ice Beam TM

## Pokemon Tier List (In-Game Viability)

### S Tier (Carry the game)
- **Typhlosion** (Cyndaquil) — Excellent speed/SpA, few bad matchups
- **Feraligatr** (Totodile) — Physical beast, Surf slave, Ice Punch
- **Red Gyarados** — Free Lv30, great stats, Dragon Dance potential
- **Ampharos** — Available early (Route 32), reliable, tanks hits
- **Espeon** — Bill's Eevee + friendship, fast special sweeper

### A Tier (Very strong picks)
- **Arcanine** — Growlithe from Route 36, good stats all around
- **Lapras** — Union Cave Friday, Ice Beam + Surf, tanky
- **Kadabra** — Abra is hard to catch but Kadabra is very strong
- **Heracross** — Headbutt trees, Megahorn is devastating
- **Nidoking** — Moon Stone evolve, great mixed movepool
- **Crobat** — Friendship from Zubat, very fast, Fly user

### B Tier (Solid choices)
- **Meganium** (Chikorita) — Defensive, but struggles in many gyms
- **Machoke/Machamp** — Fighting coverage, trade to evolve
- **Golem** — Trade to evolve, great physical stats
- **Jynx** — Ice Path, strong for Clair/Lance
- **Piloswine** — Ice Path, Ground/Ice coverage
- **Magneton** — Route 38, Steel typing is defensive

### C Tier (Usable but outclassed)
- **Pidgeot** — Early bird, Fly user, but mediocre stats
- **Raticate** — Early, fast, but fragile
- **Quagsire** — Water/Ground, only weak to Grass
- **Stantler** — Route 36, decent but forgettable

## Elite Four Team Preparation

### Recommended Levels: 42-50

### Type Needs for E4:
- **Will (Psychic)**: Dark, Ghost, Bug moves
- **Koga (Poison)**: Psychic, Ground moves
- **Bruno (Fighting)**: Flying, Psychic moves
- **Karen (Dark)**: Fighting, Bug, Fairy (N/A Gen 2) moves
- **Lance (Dragon)**: Ice (critical), Electric, Rock moves

### Essential Moves for E4:
- **Ice Beam / Blizzard** — Destroys Lance's Dragonite x3
- **Thunderbolt / Thunder** — Covers Gyarados, Charizard support
- **Earthquake** — Universal coverage
- **Psychic** — Koga's Poison types
- **Surf** — General coverage, HM required

### Key Items to Stock:
- Full Restore x20+
- Revive x10+
- Max Potion x10+
- Full Heal x10+ (Koga loves status moves)
