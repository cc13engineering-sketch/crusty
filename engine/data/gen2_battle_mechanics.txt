================================================================================
1. DAMAGE FORMULA
================================================================================

  Damage = (((2 * Level / 5 + 2) * Power * A / D / 50) * Item * Critical + 2)
           * TK * Weather * Badge * STAB * Type * MoveMod * Random * DoubleDmg

Variables:
  Level     = Attacking Pokemon's level
  Power     = Move's base power
  A         = Attacker's effective Attack (physical) or Special Attack (special)
  D         = Target's effective Defense (physical) or Special Defense (special)

Modifiers:
  Item      = 1.1 if holding a type-enhancing item (e.g. Charcoal, Mystic Water)
              1.0 otherwise

  Critical  = 2 if critical hit
              1 otherwise

  TK        = 1, 2, or 3 for successive hits of Triple Kick
              1 otherwise

  Weather   = 1.5 if Water move in Rain, or Fire move in Sun
              0.5 if Water move in Sun, or Fire move in Rain
              1.0 otherwise

  Badge     = 1.125 if player-owned Pokemon and player has the relevant badge
              1.0 otherwise
              (Zephyr Badge boosts attack types, etc.)

  STAB      = 1.5 if move type matches one of the user's types
              1.0 otherwise

  Type      = Product of type effectiveness multipliers (see type chart)
              Possible values: 0, 0.25, 0.5, 1, 2, 4

  MoveMod   = Move-specific modifiers (e.g. Rollout escalation)
              1.0 for most moves

  Random    = Random integer from 217 to 255, divided by 255
              (i.e., damage varies from ~85% to 100%)

  DoubleDmg = 2 if Pursuit hits a switching Pokemon
              1 otherwise

Critical Hit and Stat Stages:
  When a critical hit lands, if the target's Defense/SpDef stat stage is
  greater than or equal to the attacker's Attack/SpAtk stat stage, then
  ALL stat stage modifiers are ignored, burn's attack halving is ignored,
  and screen (Reflect/Light Screen) defensive boosts are ignored.
  Otherwise, the stat stages are applied normally.


================================================================================
2. CRITICAL HIT RATES
================================================================================

Critical hit stage is determined by base (0) plus modifiers:

  +1  Focus Energy or Dire Hit
  +1  Scope Lens held item
  +2  High critical-hit ratio move (Slash, Karate Chop, Crabhammer, etc.)

  Special: Farfetch'd holding Stick or Chansey holding Lucky Punch
           ignores all other stage modifiers (effectively very high rate)

Stage -> Probability:
  +0    17/256   (~6.64%)
  +1     1/8     (12.5%)
  +2     1/4     (25.0%)
  +3    85/256   (~33.2%)
  +4+    1/2     (50.0%)

Damage Multiplier: 2x normal damage

3. STAT CALCULATION
================================================================================

Gen 2 uses DVs (0-15 range) and Stat Experience (0-65535 range).
There are NO Natures in Gen 2.

HP Formula:
  HP = floor(((Base + DV) * 2 + floor(ceil(sqrt(StatExp)) / 4)) * Level / 100)
       + Level + 10

Other Stats (Atk, Def, SpAtk, SpDef, Speed):
  Stat = floor(((Base + DV) * 2 + floor(ceil(sqrt(StatExp)) / 4)) * Level / 100)
         + 5

Where:
  Base    = Species base stat value
  DV      = Determinant Value (0-15), Gen 2's equivalent of IVs
  StatExp = Stat Experience (0-65535), Gen 2's equivalent of EVs
            Accumulated by defeating Pokemon (gain = defeated species' base stat)
  Level   = Pokemon's current level

DV Details:
  - Attack DV:  0-15
  - Defense DV: 0-15
  - Speed DV:   0-15
  - Special DV: 0-15 (shared between Special Attack AND Special Defense)
  - HP DV: derived from the other 4 DVs (bit 0 of each, combined)
    HP_DV = 8*(Atk_DV % 2) + 4*(Def_DV % 2) + 2*(Spd_DV % 2) + (Spc_DV % 2)

Stat Stage Multipliers (for in-battle stages -6 to +6):
  Stage  Multiplier
  -6     2/8  = 0.25
  -5     2/7  = 0.286
  -4     2/6  = 0.333
  -3     2/5  = 0.4
  -2     2/4  = 0.5
  -1     2/3  = 0.667
   0     2/2  = 1.0
  +1     3/2  = 1.5
  +2     4/2  = 2.0
  +3     5/2  = 2.5
  +4     6/2  = 3.0
  +5     7/2  = 3.5
  +6     8/2  = 4.0

Accuracy/Evasion stages use different multipliers:
  Stage  Multiplier
  -6     3/9  = 0.333
  -5     3/8  = 0.375
  -4     3/7  = 0.429
  -3     3/6  = 0.5
  -2     3/5  = 0.6
  -1     3/4  = 0.75
   0     3/3  = 1.0
  +1     4/3  = 1.333
  +2     5/3  = 1.667
  +3     6/3  = 2.0
  +4     7/3  = 2.333
  +5     8/3  = 2.667
  +6     9/3  = 3.0

4. EXPERIENCE
================================================================================

EXP Gain Formula (defeating a Pokemon):
  EXP = b * L / 7 * (1/s) * e * a * t

Where:
  b = Base experience yield of the defeated Pokemon's species
  L = Level of the defeated Pokemon
  s = Participation/Exp Share divisor:
      - If no Exp. Share in party: s = number of Pokemon that participated
      - If Exp. Share in party: participating Pokemon get half EXP
        (s = 2 * number of participants), holders of Exp. Share split other half
  e = 1.5 if holding Lucky Egg, 1.0 otherwise
  a = 1.5 if defeated Pokemon is trainer-owned, 1.0 if wild
  t = 1.5 if Pokemon is traded (different OT), 1.0 if original trainer

Experience Growth Rate Groups:
(n = level, EXP = total experience needed to reach level n)

  FAST:
    EXP(n) = 4 * n^3 / 5

  MEDIUM FAST:
    EXP(n) = n^3

  MEDIUM SLOW:
    EXP(n) = (6 * n^3 / 5) - (15 * n^2) + (100 * n) - 140

  SLOW:
    EXP(n) = 5 * n^3 / 4

  NOTE: Only these 4 groups exist in Gen 2.
        Erratic and Fluctuating were added in Gen 3.

  KNOWN BUG: Medium Slow at level 1 calculates to -54 EXP.
  Due to unsigned integers, this wraps to 16,777,162.
  This is why eggs hatch at level 5 (not level 1).

5. CATCH RATE FORMULA
================================================================================

Modified catch rate:
  a = max(floor((3 * HPmax - 2 * HPcurrent) * rate_modified / (3 * HPmax)), 1)
      + status_bonus

Where:
  HPmax         = Target Pokemon's maximum HP
  HPcurrent     = Target Pokemon's current HP
  rate_modified = Species base catch rate * ball modifier (min 1, max 255)
  status_bonus  = +10 for Sleep or Freeze
                   +0 for Paralysis, Burn, Poison (BUGGED — intended to be +5
                       but a glitch skips this check entirely)
                   +0 for no status condition

If a >= 255, the Pokemon is caught without shake checks.

Ball Multipliers (for rate_modified calculation):
  Poke Ball    1.0x
  Great Ball   1.5x
  Ultra Ball   2.0x
  Master Ball  Guaranteed catch (bypasses formula)
  Safari Ball  1.5x
  Sport Ball   1.5x

  Apricorn Balls (intended behavior — MANY ARE BUGGED in Gen 2):
    Level Ball   If player's level > wild's: varies (1x if <=, up to 8x)
    Lure Ball    3x if fishing encounter (BUGGED: checks wrong value)
    Moon Ball    4x if target evolves with Moon Stone (BUGGED: checks wrong ID)
    Friend Ball  1x catch rate, but caught Pokemon starts with 200 friendship
    Love Ball    8x if same species, opposite gender (BUGGED: checks same gender)
    Heavy Ball   +/- to catch rate based on weight (not a multiplier)
    Fast Ball    4x if species can flee (BUGGED: only works on Magnemite/Grimer/Tangela)

Shake Check:
  After calculating a, a shake probability b is looked up from a table.
  Three shake checks are performed. Each generates a random number 0-255
  and compares it to b. Pokemon is caught if all three checks pass.
  If a >= 255, catch is guaranteed (no shakes needed).

6. STATUS CONDITIONS
================================================================================

--- SLEEP ---
  Duration:   2-7 turns (randomly determined when inflicted)
  Effect:     Pokemon cannot move. Sleep counter decrements each turn.
              Pokemon wakes on the turn the counter reaches 0 and CAN act.
  Moves:      Rest sets sleep to exactly 2 turns.
              Sleep Talk / Snore can be used while asleep.

--- FREEZE ---
  Duration:   Indefinite (no automatic thaw timer)
  Thaw Rate:  20% chance to thaw at the start of each turn
  Thaw Moves: Fire-type moves used BY the frozen Pokemon thaw it.
              Being hit by a Fire-type move thaws.
              Moves: Flame Wheel (user thaws self before attacking)
  Effect:     Pokemon cannot move while frozen.

--- PARALYSIS ---
  Speed:      Reduces Speed stat to 25% of normal (multiplied by 0.25)
  Skip:       25% chance each turn of being "fully paralyzed" (cannot move)
  Notes:      Does not wear off naturally. Must be healed.

--- BURN ---
  Damage:     1/8 of max HP lost at the end of each turn (rounded down)
  Atk Reduce: Attack stat is halved (0.5x) while burned
  Notes:      Fire-type Pokemon cannot be burned.
              Only caused by Fire-type moves and Will-O-Wisp (not in Gen 2).
              Burn damage is applied at end of turn.

--- POISON ---
  Damage:     1/8 of max HP lost at the end of each turn (rounded down)
  Notes:      Poison-type and Steel-type Pokemon cannot be poisoned.
              Poison damage also applies in the overworld (1 damage per 4 steps).

--- BADLY POISONED (Toxic) ---
  Damage:     Escalates each turn: N/16 of max HP, where N starts at 1
              and increments by 1 each turn.
              Turn 1: 1/16, Turn 2: 2/16, Turn 3: 3/16, ... etc.
  Switch:     In Gen 2, switching out RESETS badly poisoned to regular poison.
              The toxic counter is lost on switch.
  Notes:      Caused by Toxic move.

--- CONFUSION ---
  Duration:   2-5 turns (randomly determined when inflicted)
  Self-Hit:   50% chance each turn of hitting self instead of using selected move
  Damage:     Calculated as a typeless physical move with 40 base power.
              Uses the confused Pokemon's own Attack and Defense stats.
              Burn halves the self-hit damage (since it's physical).
              No STAB, no type effectiveness (typeless).
  Notes:      Confusion is a volatile status (removed on switching out).
              Focus Band cannot prevent KO from confusion self-hit in Gen 2.

7. TYPE EFFECTIVENESS CHART (Generation II — 17 Types)
================================================================================

Types: Normal, Fire, Water, Electric, Grass, Ice, Fighting, Poison,
       Ground, Flying, Psychic, Bug, Rock, Ghost, Dragon, Dark, Steel

--- SUPER EFFECTIVE (2x) ---

  Normal    -> (nothing)
  Fire      -> Grass, Ice, Bug, Steel
  Water     -> Fire, Ground, Rock
  Electric  -> Water, Flying
  Grass     -> Water, Ground, Rock
  Ice       -> Grass, Ground, Flying, Dragon
  Fighting  -> Normal, Ice, Rock, Dark, Steel
  Poison    -> Grass
  Ground    -> Fire, Electric, Poison, Rock, Steel
  Flying    -> Grass, Fighting, Bug
  Psychic   -> Fighting, Poison
  Bug       -> Grass, Psychic, Dark
  Rock      -> Fire, Ice, Flying, Bug
  Ghost     -> Ghost, Psychic
  Dragon    -> Dragon
  Dark      -> Ghost, Psychic
  Steel     -> Ice, Rock

--- NOT VERY EFFECTIVE (0.5x) ---

  Normal    -> Rock, Steel
  Fire      -> Fire, Water, Rock, Dragon
  Water     -> Water, Grass, Dragon
  Electric  -> Electric, Grass, Dragon
  Grass     -> Fire, Grass, Poison, Flying, Bug, Dragon, Steel
  Ice       -> Fire, Water, Ice, Steel
  Fighting  -> Poison, Flying, Psychic, Bug, Ghost
  Poison    -> Poison, Ground, Rock, Ghost
  Ground    -> Grass, Bug
  Flying    -> Electric, Rock, Steel
  Psychic   -> Psychic, Steel
  Bug       -> Fire, Fighting, Poison, Flying, Ghost, Steel
  Rock      -> Fighting, Ground, Steel
  Ghost     -> Dark, Steel
  Dragon    -> Steel
  Dark      -> Fighting, Dark, Steel
  Steel     -> Fire, Water, Electric, Steel

--- NO EFFECT (0x / Immune) ---

  Normal    -> Ghost
  Electric  -> Ground
  Fighting  -> Ghost
  Poison    -> Steel
  Ground    -> Flying
  Psychic   -> Dark
  Ghost     -> Normal

--- NOTES ---
  Gen 2 added Dark and Steel types.
  Poison was NOT super effective against Bug in Gen 2 (changed from Gen 1).
  Bug was NOT super effective against Poison in Gen 2 (changed from Gen 1).
  Ghost -> Psychic is super effective (fixing Gen 1 bug where it did 0x).
